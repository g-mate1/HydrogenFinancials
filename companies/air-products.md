## 1. Business overview

Air Products is the world's largest hydrogen producer by volume
across merchant, on-site and SMR-based supply, operating in 50+
countries. Hydrogen is the dominant growth pillar post-FY2025
strategic reset. The company positions itself across the value
chain as a mega-project owner (NEOM 2 GW green H2, Louisiana ~750
MMcf/d blue H2, Rotterdam ammonia cracking), a merchant supplier
(700-mile US Gulf Coast hydrogen pipeline supplying 1.7 Bcf/d from
24 plants), and engineering lead for low-carbon hydrogen projects.
The strategic pivot under CEO Eduardo Menezes (appointed February
2025, replacing Seifi Ghasemi, after Mantle Ridge activist campaign
won 3 of 4 board seats) emphasises disciplined capex allocation and
de-risking the Louisiana project via a Yara partnership; NEOM
remains the core hydrogen bet.

## 5. Hydrogen-specific operational KPIs

- **Installed capacity:** World's largest merchant and on-site
  hydrogen supplier. 700-mile US Gulf Coast pipeline supplying 1.7
  Bcf/d from 24 plants. Largest single SMR ~175 MMcf/d (Texas City
  for Gulf Coast Ammonia). NEOM 2 GW PEM electrolyser ~80% complete,
  commissioning mid-2027.
- **Committed low-carbon hydrogen capex:** ~$15bn pipeline of
  projects at various stages — NEOM, Louisiana, Rotterdam, AES
  Texas, European partnerships. FY2026 capex guidance $4.0bn;
  long-term target ~$2.5bn by 2030.
- **Major projects:** NEOM Green Hydrogen Company (SA; 2 GW PEM,
  600 tpd H2, $8.4bn financing closed May 2023); Louisiana Clean
  Energy Complex (750 MMcf/d blue H2, Yara 25-year offtake, FID
  target mid-2026, 2028 start-up); Rotterdam green-ammonia import /
  cracking with Gunvor; Air Products / AES Texas ~200 tpd green H2
  (~$4bn, 2027).
- **Cancelled / paused:** Massena, New York 35 tpd green H2 exited
  February 2025 on 45V deterioration.

## 11. Key hydrogen risks

- **Louisiana execution & de-risking:** $4.5–7bn capex contingent on
  Yara FID mid-2026, Class VI CCS permits, and 45V eligibility.
- **45V tax-credit volatility:** January 2025 Treasury rules impose
  strict emissions accounting; policy risk around 2026 elections.
- **NEOM execution & geopolitical:** technology deployment, labour,
  regional risk; merchant ammonia / hydrogen pricing exposure.
- **Activist / board pressure:** Mantle Ridge board seats force
  capex discipline; further project reviews likely.
- **Green H2 market exposure:** limited long-term fixed-price
  offtakes on NEOM and AES Texas volumes.
- **Capex reduction vs hydrogen ambition:** FY2026 $4bn capex
  target, long-term $2.5bn — limited headroom for new greenfield.
