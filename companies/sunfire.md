## 1. Business overview

Sunfire GmbH is a privately held German electrolyser manufacturer
headquartered in Dresden, with a Solingen manufacturing facility
acquired via the 2021 IHT (Industrie Haute Technologie) takeover
for pressurised alkaline. The company produces two complementary
technologies: **solid-oxide electrolysis cells (SOEC)** operating
at ~850 °C with up to 84% electrical efficiency (LHV, AC), and
**pressurised alkaline electrolysers**. Key deployments include the
Multi-PLHY project at Neste Rotterdam (2.6 MW SOEC — the world's
largest at commissioning); RWE GET H2 Nukleus (Lingen, Germany;
10 MW pilot live August 2024, 100 MW commissioned for 2027); Bad
Lauchstädt Energy Park (30 MW alkaline); and a 20 MW alkaline unit
in Finland (operational February 2025).

## 5. Hydrogen-specific operational KPIs

- **Operating / committed SOEC capacity:** Multi-PLHY Neste
  Rotterdam 2.6 MW (live since October 2023, ~60+ kg/h H2, 12
  modular stacks); Salzgitter GrInHy2.0 720 kW SOEC pilot
  completed 2022.
- **Operating / committed alkaline capacity:** RWE Lingen 10 MW
  pilot (live August 2024); Bad Lauchstädt Energy Park 30 MW
  (stacks delivered Q1 2025; operational target Q4 2025); Finland
  20 MW (operational February 2025); RWE GET H2 Nukleus 100 MW
  (targeted 2027).
- **Order backlog:** Reported >800 MW at end-2024 across SOEC and
  alkaline orders.
- **Manufacturing capacity:** Solingen alkaline facility in series
  production (nameplate not disclosed); SOEC pilot production in
  Dresden.
- **Customer base:** Neste, RWE, TotalEnergies (Leuna via Bad
  Lauchstädt), Finnish customer, Salzgitter.

## 11. Key risks

- **Private-funding cycle risk** — more raises likely needed to
  fund Solingen scale-up.
- **SOEC commercialisation** still early-stage vs. Topsoe, Bloom
  Energy and emerging SOFC/SOEC players.
- **Alkaline scale-up execution** at Solingen and customer sites
  (Bad Lauchstädt, RWE).
- **Customer concentration** (RWE, TotalEnergies, Neste).
- **Regulatory / subsidy dependence** (EU Clean Hydrogen
  Partnership, IPCEI, German state aid).
- **Management continuity** following July 2024 CEO transition.
- **Capital requirements for GW-scale production** — IPO or
  strategic investor optionality still unresolved.
