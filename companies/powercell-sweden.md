## 1. Business overview

PowerCell Sweden AB develops, manufactures and sells PEM fuel cell
stacks and integrated systems for marine, stationary and automotive
applications. The company operates as an OEM-focused supplier, with
strategic partnerships including Robert Bosch GmbH (supply and China
licensing agreement, SEK 62.2m) and Hitachi Energy (commercial order
for the Hyflex off-grid hydrogen generator). PowerCell commands
approximately 80–85% market share in its addressed marine fuel cell
segment; the installed base includes series production for Norwegian
integrator SEAM and a multi-unit Italian marine OEM order for 56
Marine System 225 units valued at SEK 165m.

The Marine System 225 (launched June 2024) is a third-generation
containerised 225 kW architecture, with 75+ units ordered since launch.
Approximately 80% of 2024 revenue is attributable to marine systems,
with additional revenue from stationary systems and royalty/licensing
income. The company is developing a methanol-fuelled M2Power250 and a
PS200 stationary platform.

PowerCell transitioned from research-stage to commercial-stage during
2023–2024. Manufacturing is centred in Gothenburg; the company is
ramping production to meet multi-year visibility from marine OEM
contracts.

## 5. Hydrogen-specific operational KPIs

- **Installed / shipped capacity:** Marine System 225 delivers 225 kW
  per containerised unit; multi-unit stacking to MW scale. 75+ units
  on order since June 2024 launch; largest single order 56 units
  (SEK 165m) to Italian marine OEM. ~80–85% share of addressed marine
  fuel cell segment.
- **Order backlog:** SEK 165m firm Italian OEM contract plus SEAM and
  stationary/methanol system orders; 75+ units disclosed as of Q1 2025.
- **Book-to-bill ratio (FY2024):** Not disclosed; Q1 2025 revenue
  74.1 MSEK with order visibility extending into 2026–2027.
- **Manufacturing capacity:** Nameplate not disclosed; ramp underway in
  Gothenburg with potential contract manufacturing partnerships.
- **Announced large contracts (past 12 months):** SEK 165m Italian
  marine OEM order; SEK 62.2m Bosch China licensing and royalty deal;
  Hitachi Energy commercial Hyflex order; SEK 50m debt facility
  (Q2 2024); SEK 182m equity raise (late 2024).

## 6. Balance sheet and liquidity

- **Cash runway (months):** ~16+ months at Q1 2025 burn vs cash of
  ~399 MSEK; positive operating cash flow and first positive 12-month
  EBITDA (SEK 27.4m) achieved in Q2 2025 reduces runway concerns.
- **Most recent capital raise:** Directed share issue of SEK 182m
  completed end-2024; 5.75m shares issued.
- **Convertible / debt instruments outstanding:** SEK 50m term loan
  facility (Q2 2024); total liabilities ~140.5m at end-2024;
  debt/equity ~4.93%.
- **Going-concern language in latest filing:** Not explicitly flagged;
  equity raise, positive operating cash flow and first positive rolling
  12m EBITDA in Q2 2025 suggest no material concern.
