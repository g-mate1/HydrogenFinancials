## 1. Business overview

PowerCell Sweden AB develops, manufactures and sells PEM fuel cell
stacks and integrated systems for marine, stationary and automotive
applications. It operates as an OEM-focused supplier, with strategic
partnerships including Robert Bosch GmbH (supply agreement and
2025 China licensing) and Hitachi Energy (Hyflex off-grid hydrogen
generator). PowerCell commands an estimated 80–85% share of its
addressed marine fuel cell segment; the installed base includes
series production for Norwegian integrator SEAM and a 56-unit
Italian marine OEM contract (SEK 165m) delivered by end-2025.

The Marine System 225 (launched June 2024) is a third-generation
containerised 225 kW architecture with 75+ units ordered since
launch. Approximately 80% of 2024 revenue is attributable to marine
systems. PowerCell is developing the methanol-fuelled M2Power250 and
the PS200 stationary platform, and entered aviation via ZeroAvia
following AS9100 certification in 2024. Manufacturing is centred in
Gothenburg.

## 11. Key risks

1. **Large marine customer concentration** — Italian OEM 56-unit
   contract was ~49% of FY2024 revenue.
2. **ZeroAvia aviation partnership dependency** — contingent on
   aircraft certification over multi-year timelines.
3. **Bosch China execution and licensing cliff** — SEK 62.2m Q2
   payment was one-time; recurring royalty depends on Bosch's
   non-automotive ramp.
4. **FX (SEK) volatility** — costs SEK-denominated; revenue
   increasingly EUR and USD; unhedged swing risk of 2–4pp on gross
   margin.
5. **Manufacturing and supplier constraints** — Gothenburg ramp
   depends on BoP and membrane supply.
6. **Product execution risk** — PS200 (2026 target) and M2Power250
   carry technical risk; delays would constrain diversification.
