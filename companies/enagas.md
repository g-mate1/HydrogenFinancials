## 1. Business overview

Enagás S.A. is Spain's Technical System Operator for natural-gas
transmission, operating ~12,000 km of pipelines, three underground
storage facilities and eight regasification plants. The company is
repositioning as Europe's hydrogen infrastructure champion via two
flagship projects: the **Spanish Hydrogen Backbone** (~2,700 km
across multiple axes including Cantabrian Coast, Ebro Valley, Levante
and the Vía de la Plata Axis linked to the Puertollano Hydrogen
Valley) and the **H2Med / BarMar corridor** (400 km offshore
Barcelona–Marseille, 2 Mt H2/yr capacity, targeted COD 2032). Both
projects are PCI-designated. Enagás also owns Scale Green Energy,
developing hydrogen refuelling stations. By 2030, hydrogen-regulated
assets are expected to exceed natural-gas assets, driven by ~€3.1bn
of hydrogen capex within a ~€4.0bn total investment plan through 2030.

## 10. Hydrogen capex & infrastructure projects

### Spanish Hydrogen Backbone
- **Scope:** ~2,700 km in two phases (Cantabrian Coast / Ebro Valley /
  Levante Ph1 ~1,500 km + Vía de la Plata Ph2 ~1,250 km), plus two
  associated underground storage facilities. Extension routes add
  ~1,480 km.
- **Capex:** €3,125m allocated within €4,035m total capex plan
  through 2030; FID targeted end-2027; COD early–mid 2030s.
- **Status:** conceptual design complete; basic engineering awarded
  for first two compressor stations (2024); CEF €24m (2025);
  regulatory framework (CNMC tariff methodology, RED III and
  Hydrogen & Decarbonised Gases Directive transposition) expected
  2026.

### H2Med / BarMar
- **Scope:** 400 km offshore pipeline Barcelona–Fos-sur-Mer /
  Marseille; 2 Mt H2/yr capacity; Enagás 50%, NaTran 33.3%, Teréga
  16.7%.
- **Capex (Enagás share):** ~€1.25bn of total ~€2.5bn corridor
  investment.
- **Timeline:** FID targeted end-2027; COD 2032; geophysical
  prospecting and technical feasibility complete in 2024; ~€35m+
  CEF design funding awarded.

### Scale Green Energy (ECOhynet)
- ~€8.2m CEF funding (100% of request); 6 refuelling stations under
  development; target 12 by 2030 (~15% of Spain's National Action
  Framework).

## 11. Key hydrogen risks

- **Regulatory framework timing (CNMC, Spain):** transport tariff
  methodology and cost-recovery rules not yet finalised; delays
  push FID/COD.
- **Cross-border coordination (FR–ES–DE):** tariff harmonisation
  and permits remain in negotiation.
- **PCI / CEF funding continuity:** dependent on EU budget cycles
  and project milestones.
- **Customer demand ramp-up:** Spanish backbone relies on forecast
  2 Mt H2/yr production by 2030; underperformance of Iberian
  producers/offtakers would erode utilisation.
- **Import competition and H2 market evolution:** Iberian export-hub
  thesis depends on French / German demand.
- **Technology & cost assumptions:** repurposing natural-gas lines
  may be costlier than modelled.
