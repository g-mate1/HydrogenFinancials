## 1. Business overview

Siemens Energy is a global energy technology and decarbonisation
company with four segments: Gas Services, Grid Technologies,
Transformation of Industry (ToI) and Siemens Gamesa. The company
employs ~100,000 and generated EUR 39.1bn in FY2025 revenue.
Hydrogen operations centre on the Silyzer 300 PEM electrolyser
platform, produced via a 74.9% Siemens Energy / 25.1% Air Liquide
joint venture operating a Berlin gigafactory (opened November 2023,
targeting 3 GW/year nameplate by 2026). Gas Services already offers
gas turbines rated for up to 75% hydrogen blending with 100%
capability targeted by 2030; Grid Technologies supports renewable-
plus-electrolysis infrastructure; Siemens Gamesa is developing an
offshore wind-to-hydrogen pilot.

## 5. Hydrogen-specific operational KPIs

- **Installed / shipped capacity:** Siemens Energy–Air Liquide JV
  passed 1.0 GW of cumulative electrolyser order intake by
  September 2025. Projects include Normand'Hy 200 MW (9 of 12 stacks
  delivered), EWE 280 MW (FID 2024, online 2027), OMV 140 MW
  (Austria), Cepsa 400 MW (Spain — southern Europe's largest).
- **Order backlog (H2 focus):** ~1 GW hydrogen backlog embedded in
  the EUR 146bn group backlog (not itemised separately).
- **Manufacturing capacity:** Berlin gigafactory — ~2 GW/year by end
  2024, target 3 GW/year by end 2026.
- **Announced large contracts (past 12 months):** EWE 280 MW, OMV
  140 MW, Cepsa 400 MW; Intermountain Power Agency (Utah) hydrogen
  energy storage study.

## 10. Hydrogen segment split

Hydrogen electrolysis sits wholly within **Transformation of
Industry**, which reported FY2025 revenue of ~€5.4bn (~13.8% of
group) at 11.3% PBSI margin. Siemens Energy does **not** separately
disclose hydrogen revenue, orders or EBITA; hydrogen is embedded
within ToI (which also includes industrial steam turbines,
compression, EAD). Hydrogen-specific cumulative revenue has been
guided to "over €1 billion" over a multi-year period.

**Hydrogen-related exposure in other segments:**
- **Gas Services** (FY2025 ~€12.5bn, 35.8% of group): hydrogen-capable
  turbines up to 75% H2 blend.
- **Grid Technologies** (FY2025 ~€15.1bn, 43.3%): grid + data-centre
  power infrastructure underpinning electrolysis projects.

**Air Liquide JV status:** 74.9% Siemens Energy / 25.1% Air Liquide;
no restructuring announced late 2024; Berlin gigafactory is the
exclusive Silyzer stack production platform.

## 11. Key hydrogen risks

- **Electrolyser margin compression:** JV in 1–3 GW ramp phase;
  stack manufacturing costs elevated; competition from Plug, ITM,
  Elogen, Enapter.
- **Air Liquide JV dependency:** 25.1% minority stake constrains
  profit capture and strategy flexibility.
- **Customer FID delays & project cancellations:** wave of 2024–25
  cancellations (California Hydrogen Hub, Repsol 350 MW freeze,
  Shell Aukra halted) creates intake volatility.
- **Siemens Gamesa drag on group profitability:** losses narrowing
  but still largest drag; breakeven targeted FY2026.
- **Stack durability & warranty claims:** PEM long-term operational
  performance still maturing.
- **Blue H2 and alternative technology competition:** AEM and SOEC
  alternatives could capture share if PEM costs stall.
