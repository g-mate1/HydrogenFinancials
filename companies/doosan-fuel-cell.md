## 1. Business overview

Doosan Fuel Cell Co., Ltd (incorporated 2019) designs and
manufactures stationary fuel-cell systems for power generation and
combined heat-and-power (CHP). The company operates two
technology platforms: Phosphoric Acid Fuel Cells (PAFC), produced
under licence from UTC Power via HyAxiom (Doosan's Connecticut
subsidiary — the PureCell® M400 platform); and Solid Oxide Fuel
Cells (SOFC), produced under a multi-year Ceres Power manufacturing
licence commenced 2020, with **mass production launched July 2025**
at a 50 MW/year Gunsan facility. The primary customer base is the
Korean Hydrogen Portfolio Standard (HPS) and Clean Hydrogen
Portfolio Standard (CHPS) auction framework operated by KEPCO /
KEA; international markets include the US, China (ZKRG master
licence) and emerging UK / Australia projects. Doosan sits at the
end-use segment of the hydrogen value chain.

## 5. Hydrogen-specific operational KPIs

- **PAFC cumulative deployment (FY2024):** 526.7 MW (1,197 units)
  in Korea; 51.9 MW (117 units) in the US / UK. Under construction:
  171.2 MW PAFC in Korea (390 units); 15.5 MW (36 units) in US / UK.
- **SOFC:** 50 MW/year nameplate at Gunsan (Jeollabuk-do); mass
  production commenced July 2025.
- **Manufacturing capacity:** PAFC ~275–300 MW/year at Iksan
  (~680 units/year). Combined ~325–350 MW/year group nameplate.
- **Order backlog dynamics:** April 2025 — **three PAFC supply
  contracts worth ~KRW 818bn (~USD 560m) terminated** due to
  project-financing delays. September 2025 — USD 308m 20-year
  service deal. November 2025 — KRW 96.4bn KEPCO PPA. November
  2025 — SK ecoplant / Hyosung Heavy Industries Korean data-centre
  cooperation. February 2026 — LG Electronics waste-heat recovery
  MOU.

## 11. Key risks

- **Korean HPS/CHPS auction-price volatility** directly affects
  customer FID and Doosan's order visibility.
- **Ceres Power royalty outflow** on every SOFC unit produced.
- **PAFC-contract-termination precedent** highlights execution risk
  in large Korean projects.
- **Natural-gas PAFC green-credential mismatch** vs evolving CHPS
  criteria.
- **Export-market dependency** (US, China) with limited
  diversification.
- **Parent-company overhang** as Doosan Corporation subsidiary.
