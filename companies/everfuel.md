# Everfuel (EFUEL)

**Headquarters:** Herning, Denmark
**Listing:** Previously Euronext Growth Oslo, ticker EFUEL;
**delisted 2 January 2025** after Faro BidCo (Swiss Life Asset
Management) acquired 92.46% in a voluntary offer at NOK 13 per
share; majority privately held since.
**Reporting currency:** EUR (previously NOK)
**Fiscal year end:** 31 December
**Segment:** Nordic pure-play green hydrogen production, distribution
and retail.
**Data last updated:** 2026-04-13
**Latest full fiscal year reported:** FY2023 (as a public company)
**Latest quarter reported:** Q2 2024 (28 August 2024)

## 1. Business overview

Everfuel A/S is a Nordic pure-play hydrogen company operating across
production (electrolysers purchased from Nel ASA), distribution
(compression, tube trailers) and retail refuelling stations,
serving commercial fleet operators, industrial customers and
hydrogen mobility. The company was spun out of Nel ASA and has
operational presence in Denmark, Norway, Sweden, the Netherlands
and Germany. Flagship asset: **HySynergy Phase 1** — a 20 MW
electrolyser in Fredericia, Denmark (joint venture with Crossbridge
Energy; ~8 t H2/day). The company was privatised in late 2024 by
Faro BidCo (Swiss Life AM subsidiary) at NOK 13/share, delisted
from Euronext Growth Oslo effective 2 January 2025, and since
November 2024 has operated within the Faro BidCo/HyVC ownership
structure alongside ITOCHU and Osaka Gas.

## 5. Hydrogen-specific operational KPIs

- **HySynergy Phase 1:** 20 MW electrolyser (Nel ASA-delivered);
  ~8 t H2/day capacity. First commercial H2 delivery to Crossbridge
  Energy completed November 2024.
- **Distribution fleet:** 12 hydrogen tube trailers; after Q2 2024
  remedial work, 7–10 units operational, targeting 10 by year-end.
- **Retail:** Operational station at Heinenoord (Netherlands)
  supplying commercial fleets.
- **Hy24 joint venture:** €200m Clean H2 Infrastructure Fund
  commitment for Nordic projects, 51% Everfuel / 49% Hy24-managed
  fund.
- **HySynergy Phase 2 (proposal):** 300 MW Fredericia expansion
  (not yet funded or approved).

## 6. Balance sheet and liquidity

- **Cash runway:** Q2 2024 cash of €13.1m (€4.0m restricted)
  insufficient to cover H2 2024 capex and operations without
  additional equity; 3–6 months of runway on cash-burn basis.
- **Most recent capital raise:** ~€20–30m private placement (H1
  2023); ongoing JV capital calls under the Hy24 structure.
- **Convertible / debt instruments outstanding:** Not disclosed;
  capital structure primarily equity and JV financing.
- **Going-concern / material uncertainty:** Effectively material.
  The Q2 2024 release explicitly stated that cash "not expected to
  cover all planned activities the next 12 months" without further
  capital injection, and only €9.1m of the €13.1m Q2 2024 cash
  balance was unrestricted — implying a 3–6 month runway at the
  then-current burn. This liquidity stress was a direct precursor
  to the Faro BidCo (Swiss Life AM) voluntary offer in September
  2024 and the subsequent privatisation / delisting. Treat the
  risk profile as on par with other distressed hydrogen pure-plays
  such as McPhy and AFC Energy.

## 7. Notable recent events (past 12 months)

- **September–December 2024:** Faro BidCo ApS (Swiss Life AM
  subsidiary) acquired 92.46% of Everfuel at NOK 13/share; Everfuel
  delisted from Euronext Growth Oslo effective 2 January 2025.
- **2024:** Repeated HySynergy Phase 1 commissioning delays; first
  hydrogen delivery to Crossbridge Energy in November 2024.
- **December 2024:** Strategic partnership with ITOCHU and Osaka
  Gas signed via HyVC ApS to accelerate Nordic electrolyser
  capacity.
- **2023–24:** Hy24 JV ongoing; HySynergy Phase 1 acquired by the
  joint venture at €26.9m transaction value (debt-free valuation
  €44.9m); expected further capital injections.
- **H1 2024:** Guided to €11–15m net loss for FY2024; flagged
  liquidity tightness beyond Q3 2024 without additional capital.
- **H1 2024:** Seven of 12 hydrogen distribution trailers flagged
  with systematic technical failures; remedial programme under way.

## 10. Segment / revenue mix (FY2023 estimates)

Everfuel does not publish formal segment P&L; the below is based
on public commentary:
- **Production (HySynergy 20 MW + other pilots):** minimal revenue
  until full commissioning; ~€2–3m.
- **Distribution (tube trailers, fleet refuelling):** ~€2–3m, plus
  related compressed-H2 sales.
- **Retail (Heinenoord + other Dutch stations):** limited throughput;
  demonstration / customer-development activity.
- **Geographic mix:** majority Denmark; Netherlands (Heinenoord);
  Germany; Norway and Sweden via Hy24.
- **Construction / project income (JV management):** ~€2–3m.

## 11. Key risks

- **Hydrogen mobility demand weakness** across Nordic / NL / DE
  fleets.
- **Refuelling station utilisation risk:** fixed operating costs
  not yet offset by H2 sales volumes.
- **HySynergy Phase 1 offtake risk:** Crossbridge Energy offtake
  terms not fully disclosed; dependent on refinery cycles and
  renewable power economics.
- **Severe shareholder dilution** historically; privatisation
  reflects significant dilution of original public investors.
- **Reliance on government subsidies and carbon pricing:** H2 pilot
  economics depend on EU support schemes.
- **Operational / execution risk:** repeated commissioning delays,
  trailer fleet defects, stretched management bandwidth with ~75
  FTE and multi-country operations.

## 12. Upcoming catalysts

- **HySynergy Phase 1 full production ramp (Q4 2024 – Q1 2025).**
- **FY2024 results (Mar 2025)** — confirmation of loss range and
  capital adequacy.
- **HySynergy Phase 2 (300 MW Fredericia)** — binding contracts or
  FID indications in H1 2025 / 2026.
- **Hy24 JV acquisition pipeline** — additional Nordic hydrogen
  infrastructure deals.
- **ITOCHU / Osaka Gas collaboration milestones (2025–26).**
- **EU hydrogen market and mandate evolution** (RED III, industrial

- **Private-equity exit timeline:** Faro BidCo (Swiss Life AM) would
  typically target a 5–7 year hold; a future re-IPO or secondary
  sale could occur if Phase 2 and commercial scale are achieved.
