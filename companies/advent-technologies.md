**Headquarters:** East Hartford, Connecticut, USA (R&D in Patras,
Greece)
**Listing:** Previously NASDAQ: ADN; **delisted 30 October 2025**;
now trades OTC as ADNH (warrants: ADNWW).
**Reporting currency:** USD
**Fiscal year end:** 31 December
**Segment:** High-temperature PEM (HT-PEM) fuel cells and MEAs for
defence, aviation, maritime and stationary power.
**Data last updated:** 2026-04-13
**Latest full fiscal year reported:** FY2024
**Latest quarter reported:** Q2 FY2025 (30 June 2025)

## 1. Business overview

Advent Technologies Holdings develops HT-PEM fuel cells and
membrane-electrode assemblies (MEAs) for defence, aviation, marine
and stationary power applications. Flagship product: **Honey Badger
50 (HB50)** portable methanol-to-power fuel cell for US Department
of Defense use. R&D and prototype manufacturing is concentrated at
Patras Science Park (Greece), with pilot facilities in Livermore
(California) and East Hartford (Connecticut). In August 2025 Advent
acquired exclusive rights to Ion Pair HT-PEM membrane technology
from Los Alamos National Laboratory for marine, aviation and
portable power applications up to 160 lbs. In April 2026 Advent
signed a licence and joint-development agreement with EH Group
Engineering AG for stationary-power HT-PEM stacks. Partnerships
include development agreements with Airbus, Siemens Energy and
Hyundai, plus ongoing US Army contracts.
