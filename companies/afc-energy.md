## 1. Business overview

AFC Energy develops decentralised alkaline fuel cell generators and
ammonia cracking systems for zero-emission hydrogen production.
Core products: H-Power generators (30 kW and 200 kW) and the Hy-5
containerised ammonia cracker (up to 500 kg/day, targeting £10/kg
cost by end-2026 under a "fuel as a service" model). Addressable
markets: temporary power for construction sites (displacing
diesel), EV charging infrastructure, maritime decarbonisation and
data-centre backup. Customers have included Speedy Hire (50/50 JV
Speedy Hydrogen Solutions, 20 units delivered in FY2024), Acciona
(Madrid 45 kVA), ABB (historic £4m order; ongoing EV charging
platform), Ricardo (marine / stationary engineering), Komatsu (USD
2m JDA February 2026). Two 50/50 joint ventures: Speedy Hydrogen
Solutions (winding down) and an Industrial Chemicals Group
partnership. The company is proposing a name change to H-Power plc
at the April 2026 AGM.

## 10. Segment / revenue mix

- **H-Power generators (30 kW & 200 kW S/S+):** stationary zero-
  emission power for construction sites, maritime, data centres,
  EV charging. FY2024 revenue £4.0m (20 units to Speedy Hydrogen
  Solutions JV); minimal FY2025 (pivot away from B2B unit sales).
- **Hy-5 ammonia cracker (fuel as a service):** containerised
  on-site H2 production; £10/kg target; pilot 400 kg/day;
  production 500 kg/day; main commercialisation vehicle from 2026
  via JV partnerships.
- **Fuel cell stacks:** sub-system sales to integrators (ABB,
  Ricardo pilots); ~250 stacks/year current capacity; 85% cost
  reduction achieved.
- **Pilot facility / licensing:** transfer of pilot ammonia cracker
  to Industrial Chemicals Group JV (Q4 2025); JV dividends expected
  H1 2026.
- **Grant-funded R&D:** ~89% of R&D eligible for UK R&D tax credits;
  £2.7m claimed FY24; FY25 claims pending.

## 11. Key risks

- **Going-concern uncertainty:** material going-concern doubt
  disclosed in H1 FY25; additional capital raise likely required
  Q3/Q4 2026.
- **Dilution from serial equity raises:** five rounds since 2020;
  further £20–30m raises plausible by 2027–28.
- **ABB / OEM partnership execution risk:** flagship £4m S Series
  order from ABB partially fulfilled; broader EV-charging platform
  still in development.
- **Pilot-to-commercial conversion risk:** Hy-5 first-of-a-kind; ICG
  JV dependent on timely asset transfer and ramp-up.
- **Alkaline vs PEM competitive risk:** PEM ecosystem is
  significantly ahead in automotive; AFC's cost advantage may not
  offset ecosystem lock-in.
- **Timing & commercialisation uncertainty:** multiple dependencies
  (Hy-5 delivery, ICG ramp, Komatsu engine integration, Speedy Hire
  wind-down) with stretched management bandwidth.
