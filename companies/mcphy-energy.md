# McPhy Energy (MCPHY)

**Headquarters:** Belfort, France
**Listing:** Euronext Growth Paris, ALMCP (suspended 8 July 2025;
delisting in progress)
**Reporting currency:** EUR
**Fiscal year end:** 31 December
**Segment:** Alkaline electrolyser manufacturer and hydrogen
production equipment provider (in liquidation).
**Data last updated:** 2026-04-13
**Latest full fiscal year reported:** FY2024
**Latest quarter reported:** FY2024 full year (31 March 2025)

## 1. Business overview

McPhy Energy S.A. was a French designer and manufacturer of alkaline
water electrolysers for large-scale hydrogen production. The company
operated across the hydrogen value chain, positioned primarily in
production equipment supply, with the McLyzer range (1 MW to
multi-MW units) and the modular Piel product line. Manufacturing was
centred on the Belfort Gigafactory (commissioned H1 2024, design
capacity 1 GW/year) with legacy production at San Miniato, Italy.
The hydrogen refuelling station business (Atawey) was divested in
2024. In 2025 McPhy entered conciliation, then judicial
reorganisation, and finally judicial liquidation. On 8 July 2025 the
Belfort Commercial Court approved John Cockerill Hydrogen as the
buyer of the majority of McPhy's assets (Belfort Gigafactory,
technologies, IP, and 51 of 71 French employees). Shares were
suspended the same day.

## 6. Balance sheet and liquidity

- **Cash runway:** "sufficient flexibility until end of H1 2025"
  per March 2025 disclosure; exhausted quickly.
- **Most recent capital raise:** €30m OCEANEs to EDF Pulse Holding
  and EPIC Bpifrance (June 2024); €16m lease financing for Belfort
  Gigafactory.
- **Convertible / debt instruments outstanding:** €30m OCEANEs;
  €16m Belfort lease; ~€4.9m traditional debt (2023).
- **Going-concern language in latest filing:** Yes — material
  uncertainty flagged in the 2024 annual report.

## 10. Asset disposition

**Acquired by John Cockerill Hydrogen:** Belfort Gigafactory (1 GW
nameplate), full McLyzer / Piel technology portfolio, IP, 51 of 71
French employees, ongoing Belfort contracts and customer lists.

**Liquidated separately / not transferred:** San Miniato (Italy)
legacy facility, residual inventory and work-in-progress, Atawey
(pre-divested June 2024), administrative overhead assets, remaining
cash and financial investments after creditor distributions.

**Creditor structure:** €30m OCEANE convertibles (EDF Pulse,
Bpifrance); €16m lease obligations; unsecured trade creditors;
employee and tax liabilities. Shareholders expected to receive nil.

## 11. Cautionary lessons

1. **Gigafactory overbuild vs. demand realisation** — commissioning
   Belfort into a weak order pipeline burdened fixed costs at <5%
   nameplate utilisation.
2. **Project / order execution delays (2023–2024)** — key customer
   contracts slipped; 24 MW central-Europe deal cancelled October
   2024.
3. **Accelerating cash burn without equity cushion** — operating
   cash burn €51.4m (2023) then €64m (2024); €30m OCEANE injection
   in June 2024 proved insufficient.
4. **Offtake and customer concentration** — pilot-scale customers
   without committed hydrogen demand.
5. **Market timing & subsidy dependency** — commissioning at peak
   capex cycle as global electrolyser overcapacity emerged.
6. **Going-concern erosion** — equity fell €88.2m → €19.9m in six
   months; FY2024 net loss €74.1m driven by impairments.

## 12. Current status (2026-04-13)

- **Trading & listing:** Shares (ALMCP) suspended 8 July 2025;
  delisting from Euronext Growth Paris requested the same day;
  formal delisting proceedings ongoing. All-time low €0.05 (26 June
  2025); all-time high €41.70 (15 January 2021).
- **Judicial status:** McPhy Energy S.A. remains in judicial
  liquidation under Maître Flavien Marchal, Belfort Commercial Court.
- **Residual market cap pre-suspension:** ~€2m; shares effectively
  worthless, with nil expected shareholder recovery.
- **Residual assets:** Belfort Gigafactory, IP and core personnel
  transferred to John Cockerill Hydrogen; San Miniato status
  unclear; corporate HQ and remaining liabilities managed by
  liquidator.

Key takeaway: McPhy's collapse from >€1.4bn peak market cap (2021)
to nil shareholder value (2025) is a cautionary case of
capital-intensive scale-up outpacing commercialisation velocity in
emerging hydrogen electrolyser markets. The July 2025 John Cockerill
acquisition preserved the Belfort asset and core IP but left
shareholders with nothing.
