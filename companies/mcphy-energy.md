## 1. Business overview

McPhy Energy S.A. was a French designer and manufacturer of alkaline
water electrolysers for large-scale hydrogen production. The company
operated across the hydrogen value chain, positioned primarily in
production equipment supply, with its McLyzer range (1 MW to multi-MW
units) and the modular Piel product line.

Customer base spanned industrial gases, chemicals, refining, steel and
hydrogen mobility operators across Europe and North America.
Manufacturing was centred on the Belfort Gigafactory (commissioned H1
2024, design capacity 1 GW/year) with legacy production at San
Miniato, Italy. The company divested its hydrogen refuelling station
business (Atawey) in 2024 to focus on electrolyser core operations.

In 2025 McPhy entered conciliation, then judicial reorganisation, and
finally judicial liquidation. On 8 July 2025 the Belfort Commercial
Court approved John Cockerill Hydrogen as the buyer of the majority of
McPhy's assets (Belfort Gigafactory, technologies, IP, and 51 of 71
French employees). Shares were suspended the same day.

## 5. Hydrogen-specific operational KPIs

- **Installed / shipped capacity:** Major H1 2024 installations: 4 MW
  Plansee (Austria), 4 MW AAK (Sweden), 1+1 MW ArcelorMittal/VEO
  (Germany). 16 MW unit delivered in early 2025 for CEOG project.
  Electrolyser segment was 92% of restated 2024 revenue.
- **Order backlog:** €29.8m at 31 Dec 2024 (+25% YoY); electrolyser
  sub-backlog €23.7m.
- **Book-to-bill ratio (FY2024):** ~2.26x at group level; ~1.50x for
  electrolysers alone.
- **Manufacturing capacity:** Belfort Gigafactory 1 GW/year nameplate
  (combined group 1.3 GW/year with San Miniato). XL stack production
  was planned for H2 2025, first commercial production H1 2026 —
  overtaken by liquidation and acquisition.
- **Announced large contracts (past 12 months prior to liquidation):**
  Plansee 4 MW green metal project; ArcelorMittal/VEO low-carbon steel
  partnership; AAK 4 MW; CEOG 16 MW.

## 6. Balance sheet and liquidity

- **Cash runway (months):** Explicitly stated "sufficient flexibility
  until end of H1 2025" (March 2025 disclosure). Runway deteriorated
  quickly; judicial proceedings initiated April 2025.
- **Most recent capital raise:** €30m OCEANEs (convertible bonds) to
  EDF Pulse Holding (€15m) and EPIC Bpifrance (€15m), completed June
  2024; €16m lease financing for Belfort Gigafactory.
- **Convertible / debt instruments outstanding:** €30m OCEANEs (June
  2024); €16m Belfort lease; ~€4.9m traditional debt (2023).
- **Going-concern language in latest filing:** Yes. 2024 annual report
  explicitly flagged material uncertainty on going concern.

## 7. Notable recent events (past 12 months)

- **Conciliation procedure opened (10 Apr 2025):** Belfort Commercial
  Court appointed conciliator due to cash runway ending end-June 2025.
- **Judicial reorganisation & takeover (8 Jul 2025):** Belfort Court
  appointed John Cockerill Hydrogen as buyer of the majority of
  McPhy's activities — Belfort Gigafactory, technologies, IP, 51 of
  71 French employees.
- **Judicial proceedings converted to liquidation (8 Jul 2025):** Maître
  Flavien Marchal appointed judicial liquidator. Trading suspended;
  delisting requested. Share value expected to be nil.
- **Revenue guidance downgrade (13 Jan 2025):** FY2024 guidance revised
  to ~€11m (from €18–22m) due to project delays and supply chain
  issues.
- **Hydrogen refuelling station business divested (2024):** Atawey
  divestiture to focus on electrolyser core.
- **FY2024 results (31 Mar 2025):** Revenue €13.2m; electrolyser
  segment €15.8m (+15% YoY); order intake doubled to €28.1m; net loss
  €74.1m driven by impairments and restructuring; equity collapsed
  from €88.2m to €19.9m.
