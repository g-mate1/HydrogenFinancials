## 1. Business overview

Enapter AG designs, manufactures and sells Anion Exchange Membrane
(AEM) electrolysers — a lower-cost, iridium-free alternative to PEM
and alkaline technologies. Core products are the AEM Multicore
(modular MW-scale) and the EL 4 single-core unit, paired with
proprietary AI-driven energy management software. Customers span
Europe (Italy, France, Germany), North America and increasingly
Asia via the Wolong Enapter Hydrogen Technologies joint venture
(launched February 2024, focused on Asia–Pacific). Manufacturing
is centred on an automated campus in Saerbeck, Germany with 300
MW/year nameplate capacity. Revenue is predominantly product sales,
with minor Italian government R&D grant income (~€1.3m in 2024).

## 5. Hydrogen-specific operational KPIs

- **Installed / shipped capacity:** ~11.5 MW contracted in Italy
  (regional Hydrogen Valleys); AEM Nexus 1000 (1 MW unit) in series
  production at Wolong JV from October 2024; ~250 EL 4 single-core
  units ordered across USA, Belgium, India and Germany in 2024.
- **Order backlog:** €42–45m at end of H1 2025; ~€29m allocated to
  FY2025 deliveries; order intake FY2024 €53m (+165% YoY).
- **Book-to-bill (FY2024):** >2.0x (€53m intake / €21.4m revenue).
- **Manufacturing capacity:** Saerbeck 300 MW/year nameplate (1
  unit per ~4 minutes target); Wolong JV 50 MW/year (operational
  from late 2024 but delayed).
- **Announced contracts (past 12 months):** France (270+ EL 4
  units); Italy (4 MW Multicore order from Opificio Idrogeno
  Marche); USA (270 EL 4 units via Clean H2 distributor).

## 6. Balance sheet and liquidity

- **Cash runway:** ~4–5 months at H1 2025 burn rate (EBITDA
  €−8.1m; cash €4.6m end-2024) before capital raises.
- **Most recent capital raise:** €12m oversubscribed (November
  2025), combining €2.4m equity (1.52m shares at €1.58) with €9.6m
  zero-coupon mandatory convertible. Prior: €8m equity raise
  (October 2024, 1.88m shares at €4.26).
- **Convertible / debt instruments outstanding:** Patrimonium
  Middle Market Debt Fund bearer bond €25.6m (extended to 29
  February 2028); mandatory convertible issued 2025.
- **Going-concern language in latest filing:** Yes — material
  uncertainty disclosed in FY2024 audit opinion.

## 7. Notable recent events (past 12 months)

- **H1 2025 guidance cut:** ~€20m+ of revenue reclassified into
  FY2026 following Wolong JV certification delays in China.
- **Saerbeck ramp struggles:** FY2024 gross margin compressed from
  66% to 31% on factory under-absorption.
- **Capital structure stress:** cash burned from €14.6m to €4.6m
  in FY2024; Nov 2025 oversubscribed raise (€12m total) extended
  runway but at significant dilution.
- **Wolong JV milestone:** AEM Nexus 1000 series production
  launched October 2024; 50 MW/year Chinese capacity target delayed.
- **Order backlog resilient** despite execution miss (€53m FY2024
  intake, +165% YoY).

## 11. Key risks

- **Going-concern & liquidity:** material uncertainty flagged;
  Nov 2025 raise provides only a limited runway extension.
- **Saerbeck factory ramp failure:** FY2024 gross margin collapse
  signals cost overruns or weak utilisation; risk of stranded capex.
- **AEM vs PEM / alkaline competitiveness:** cost advantage only
  realises at scale (50+ MW/year).
- **Wolong JV delays & China execution:** certification delays
  halved H1 2025 revenue guidance.
- **Customer order-to-cash conversion:** €42m backlog vs modest
  cash inflow (€5.6m H1 2025 revenue).
- **Gross margin pressure:** 31% vs 66% prior year due to product
  mix and ramp-up inefficiencies.

## 12. Upcoming catalysts

- **Saerbeck utilisation ramp (H2–Q4 2026):** >60% capacity
  utilisation would drive margin recovery.
- **Wolong JV production scale (Q3–Q4 2026):** confirmation of
  50 MW/year series production and first €10–15m revenue.
- **FY2025 full-year results (Feb 2026):** liquidity outlook and
  execution credibility.
- **Major contract wins** from European green H2 consortia or
  Italian Hydrogen Valleys Tranche 2.
- **Refinance / debt extension (2026):** Patrimonium bearer bond
  and mandatory convertible.
- **AEM cost validation** vs PEM / alkaline at commercial scale.
