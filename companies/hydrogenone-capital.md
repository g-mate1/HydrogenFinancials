**Data last updated:** 2026-04-13
**Latest full fiscal year reported:** FY2024
**Latest quarter reported:** H1 2025 (30 June 2025)

## 1. Business overview

Hydrogen Capital Growth plc (formerly HydrogenOne Capital Growth
plc; renamed 31 July 2025) is the first London-listed closed-ended
investment company dedicated to clean hydrogen. It launched in July
2021 with a £107m IPO. The portfolio comprises ~10–12 private
hydrogen companies (~95% of assets), listed hydrogen equities
(~2%) and cash (~3%). Geographic spread covers Germany, Estonia,
UK, Norway and the Nordic region. From July 2025 the company is
pursuing a managed-realisation strategy under a new Investment
Adviser (Redwheel / RWC Asset Management LLP), following removal of
the prior manager (HydrogenOne Capital LLP). Portfolio companies
generated approximately £76m in aggregate revenue over the twelve
months to 30 June 2024 (+44% YoY).

## 9. Valuation & capital structure

The trust trades at a material discount to NAV (76% at end-2024;
compressed to 33.8% by June 2025). Closed-ended structure and
illiquid private-company portfolio limit daily liquidity. Ordinary
shares only; no preference or debt instruments.

## 11. Key risks

- **Wide NAV discount and realisation slippage:** 76% at end-2024;
  wind-down execution depends on illiquid private exits.
- **Private-asset valuation uncertainty** — mark-to-model
  methodology with high discount-rate sensitivity.
- **Portfolio concentration** in top three holdings.
- **Managed wind-down execution risk** under new Redwheel
  Investment Adviser.
- **Hydrogen sector maturation delay** prolongs cash-bleed.
- **Legacy listed-equity exposure** to ITM / Nel / HydrogenPro /
  McPhy.
- **Cash depletion** — £1.2m at end August 2025, ~£0.6m quarterly
  burn.
- **Governance transition** and information-handover delays.

## 12. Investment thesis & outlook

The trust now pursues an **orderly wind-down** with Redwheel
targeting systematic asset sales and return of capital. Near-term
catalysts include portfolio-company secondary sales (Sunfire,
Cranfield, HiiROC, Elcogen) and share-price re-rating on improved
cash-burn clarity. Downside risks dominate: extended runway
pressure, illiquid-asset discounts at realisation and weak
follow-on hydrogen venture-capital appetite may drive returns
materially below historical cost. The July 2025 pivot implies the
board views the prior "growth" thesis as no longer tenable.
