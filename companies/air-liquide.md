**Headquarters:** Paris, France
**Listing:** Euronext Paris, AI
**Reporting currency:** EUR

**Segment:** Industrial gases major; hydrogen is a strategic pillar
with ~€8bn capex commitment to 2035 targeting €6bn hydrogen revenue

**Data last updated:** 2026-04-13
**Latest full fiscal year reported:** FY2025
**Latest quarter reported:** Q3 2025 (28 October 2025)

## 1. Business overview

Air Liquide is a global industrial gases major operating in 59
countries. Hydrogen is embedded in two main reportable areas: Gas &
Services / Large Industries (on-site and merchant hydrogen from SMR,
plus emerging low-carbon projects) and Engineering & Technologies
(electrolyser sales, low-carbon project engineering, Siemens Energy
gigafactory JV). Airgas (North America) provides merchant hydrogen.
Cryocap CCS pilots, the Trailblazer 20 MW Oberhausen plant, the
200 MW Normand'Hy project, the 200 MW ELYgator (Netherlands), the
250 MW Air Liquide / TotalEnergies JV, and the ExxonMobil Baytown
oxygen/nitrogen supply contract anchor the low-carbon growth
portfolio.

  Berlin gigafactory targeting 3 GW/year PEM stack output; ramping

## 10. Hydrogen segment split

**Not formally broken out.** Air Liquide does not separately disclose
hydrogen revenue. Hydrogen sits within:

1. **Large Industries** — merchant and on-site H2; FY2024 LI revenue
   +1.2%, with hydrogen volumes helped by a large new China H2 unit
   (March 2024) and higher hydrogen electricity sales in North America.
   Hydrogen share of LI: not disclosed.
2. **Engineering & Technologies** — electrolyser and low-carbon project
   engineering, including the 25.1%-owned Siemens Energy PEM gigafactory
   (3 GW/year target).
3. **Global Markets & Technologies** — minimal direct H2 exposure.

**Disclosed low-carbon H2 investment commitments:**
- ~€8bn capex through 2035 (3 GW electrolysis + SMR + CCS).
- ~€6bn hydrogen revenue target by 2035.
- Named projects: Normand'Hy 200 MW (28 kt/yr green H2, 2026–27);
  ELYgator 200 MW (2027); TotalEnergies JV 250 MW (2027–28); Baytown
  USD 850m supply; Antwerp ENHANCE 30 kt/yr (2027); Cryocap Port-Jérôme
  100 kt/yr CO2 capture.

Low-carbon / renewable H2 is currently estimated at <5% of group
revenue; material ramp expected 2026–2030.

## 11. Key hydrogen risks

- **Capex programme execution & FID delays:** Baytown (USD 850m) FID
  contingent on policy and offtake; Siemens JV stack output ramping
  behind original 3 GW/year ambition.
- **Customer FID and offtake uncertainty:** Normand'Hy tied to
  TotalEnergies Gonfreville; ELYgator to TotalEnergies tolling; broader
  European refining demand under margin pressure.
- **Siemens Energy JV minority stake:** 25.1% interest constrains
  profit capture; any Siemens Energy strategic pivot dilutes returns.
- **US 45V eligibility for blue H2:** tightening emissions-accounting
  rules add uncertainty to Baytown monetisation.
- **Merchant H2 commoditisation:** legacy SMR volumes exposed to gas-
  and crude-price cycles.
- **Electrolyser / power supply maturity:** PPA terms and green-power
  availability materially affect project-level economics.
