# FuelCell Energy (FCEL)

**Headquarters:** Danbury, Connecticut, USA
**Listing:** NASDAQ, FCEL
**Reporting currency:** USD
**Fiscal year end:** 31 October
**Segment:** Distributed power generation using carbonate fuel cell
(MCFC) technology for stationary power, grid resiliency, data centres,
and CO2 capture; solid-oxide electrolyser (SOEC) development for
hydrogen production.
**Data last updated:** 2026-04-13
**Latest full fiscal year reported:** FY2025 (ended 31 October 2025)
**Latest quarter reported:** Q1 FY2026 (ended 31 January 2026)

## 1. Business overview

FuelCell Energy designs, manufactures, develops, installs, operates
and maintains megawatt-scale stationary fuel cell power plants. The
company has approximately 1 GW of global fuel cell deployments
spanning 23+ years of manufacturing experience. Its proprietary
carbonate fuel cell (MCFC) technology generates clean electricity,
heat, hydrogen and water, with optional CO2 capture. Customers span
utilities, data centres, wastewater treatment facilities, municipal
authorities, commercial entities and microgrids across North America,
South Korea and Europe.

Manufacturing is centred in Torrington, Connecticut, with current
nameplate capacity of 100 MW and expansion scenarios under evaluation
up to 350 MW. A joint development agreement with ExxonMobil's EMTEC
targets carbonate fuel cell carbon capture, with a pilot at Esso
Nederland's Rotterdam refinery targeted for commissioning in 2026.
Parallel SOEC development at Idaho National Laboratory demonstrates
hydrogen production coupled with nuclear power (250 kW turn-key system
in validation).

Fiscal 2025 is a year of strategic realignment: workforce cut ~22%
across two restructurings (Nov 2024, June 2025), operating expense
down ~30% vs FY2024, and solid-oxide power generation development
largely discontinued to focus on electrolysis partnerships and data
centre demand. Strong data centre momentum: FY2025 revenue +41% to
$158.2m, with the business development pipeline up 275% since
February 2025.

## 5. Hydrogen-specific operational KPIs

- **Installed / shipped capacity:** ~1.0 GW global fuel cell
  deployments (stationary MCFC); Torrington facility running at
  ~41 MW annualised (100 MW current config; 350 MW expansion under
  evaluation).
- **Order backlog:** $1.19bn at 31 Oct 2025; $1.17bn at 31 Jan 2026.
  South Korea represents 100+ MW in backlog plus another 100 MW under
  MOU; includes 7.4 MW Hartford Project and long-term service
  agreements with GGE (Hwaseong-si, 58.8 MW) and CGN-Yulchon.
- **Book-to-bill ratio (FY2025):** ~7.5x ($1.19bn backlog / $158.2m
  revenue).
- **Manufacturing capacity:** 100 MW current nameplate at Torrington;
  expansion scenarios to 350 MW under evaluation.
- **Announced large contracts (past 12 months):** Hartford Project
  (7.4 MW CT); GGE Hwaseong-si LTSA (10 modules, 58.8 MW); CGN-Yulchon
  LTSA; ExxonMobil/EMTEC pilot at Esso Nederland Rotterdam refinery
  (construction Oct 2024, commissioning 2026); Idaho National
  Laboratory 250 kW SOEC demonstration.

## 6. Balance sheet and liquidity

- **Cash runway (months):** ~48–52 months at Q1 FY2026 cash ($379.6m)
  and recent quarterly burn. However, FY2025 operating cash flow
  ($125.3m) and free cash flow ($143.9m) remained deeply negative;
  reliance on ATM issuance and EXIM facility.
- **Most recent capital raise:** ATM equity offerings — 6.4m shares
  for $56.3m net in Q4 FY2025, additional 1.6m shares for $13.1m
  post-quarter; Q1 FY2026 ATM brought cash to $379.6m. EXIM credit
  agreements (2024 and 2025) with $55.0m minimum cash covenant.
- **Convertible / debt instruments outstanding:** Long-term debt
  $103.8m at end FY2025 (down from $118.0m). Debt/equity 0.16; net
  cash $174.3m. No convertible bonds disclosed.
- **Going-concern language in latest filing:** Not disclosed in
  summaries; restructuring and frequent ATM issuance signal liquidity
  management amid negative operating cash flow.

## 7. Notable recent events (past 12 months)

- **Reverse stock split (Nov 2024):** 1-for-30 effective 8 Nov 2024,
  approved 65.8% in favour, to maintain Nasdaq minimum bid price.
- **Global restructuring (Nov 2024):** ~75 employees (13%) reduced;
  discontinued solid-oxide power generation development; focus
  realigned to MCFC, data centres and SOEC partnerships.
- **Second restructuring (Jun 2025):** Additional ~22% workforce
  reduction; operating expense target increased to 30% vs FY2024;
  post-cut headcount ~426.
- **Revenue acceleration:** FY2025 +41% to $158.2m; Q4 FY2025 +12%
  YoY to $55.0m; Q1 FY2026 +61% YoY to $30.5m.
- **Gross margin improvement:** −32.0% → −16.7% FY2024 → FY2025.
- **Data centre strategy pivot:** Business development pipeline +275%
  since Feb 2025; standardised 12.5 MW packaged power blocks
  announced early 2026.
- **ExxonMobil JV:** Agreement extended through 31 Dec 2026; Rotterdam
  pilot construction began Oct 2024; lab tests show >90% CO2 capture.
- **SOEC hydrogen production:** 250 kW demonstration at Idaho National
  Laboratory in validation; targets pairing with nuclear power plants.
- **Backlog dynamics:** $1.19bn backlog +2.6% YoY (Oct 2025); Q1
  FY2026 backlog −10.8% YoY to $1.17bn reflects strong order
  conversion.
