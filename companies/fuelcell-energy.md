# FuelCell Energy (FCEL)

**Headquarters:** Danbury, Connecticut, USA
**Listing:** NASDAQ, FCEL
**Reporting currency:** USD
**Fiscal year end:** 31 October
**Segment:** Distributed power generation using carbonate fuel cell
(MCFC) technology for stationary power, grid resiliency, data
centres and CO2 capture; solid-oxide electrolyser (SOEC) development
for hydrogen production.
**Data last updated:** 2026-04-13
**Latest full fiscal year reported:** FY2025 (ended 31 October 2025)
**Latest quarter reported:** Q1 FY2026 (ended 31 January 2026)

## 1. Business overview

FuelCell Energy designs, manufactures, develops, installs, operates
and maintains megawatt-scale stationary fuel cell power plants. The
company has ~1 GW of global fuel-cell deployments spanning 23+ years
of manufacturing experience. Its proprietary carbonate fuel cell
(MCFC) technology generates clean electricity, heat, hydrogen and
water, with optional CO2 capture. Customers span utilities, data
centres, wastewater treatment facilities, municipal authorities,
commercial entities and microgrids across North America, South
Korea and Europe.

Manufacturing is centred in Torrington, Connecticut, with current
nameplate capacity of 100 MW and expansion scenarios under
evaluation up to 350 MW. A joint development agreement with
ExxonMobil's EMTEC targets carbonate fuel cell carbon capture, with
a pilot at Esso Nederland's Rotterdam refinery targeted for 2026
commissioning. Parallel SOEC development at Idaho National
Laboratory demonstrates hydrogen production coupled with nuclear
power (250 kW turn-key system in validation).

FY2025 was a year of strategic realignment: workforce cut ~22%
across two restructurings (Nov 2024, June 2025); operating expense
down ~30% vs FY2024; solid-oxide power generation development
largely discontinued to focus on electrolysis partnerships and data
centre demand. Revenue +41% to $158.2m and the business development
pipeline +275% since February 2025.

## 6. Balance sheet and liquidity

- **Cash runway:** ~48–52 months at Q1 FY2026 cash ($379.6m) and
  current burn; reliance on ATM issuance and EXIM facility.
- **Most recent capital raise:** ATM equity offerings — 6.4m
  shares for $56.3m net in Q4 FY2025; 1.6m shares for $13.1m
  post-quarter; Q1 FY2026 ATM brought cash to $379.6m. EXIM 2024/
  2025 credit agreements with $55.0m minimum cash covenant.
- **Convertible / debt instruments outstanding:** $103.8m long-term
  debt; debt/equity 0.16. No convertibles disclosed.
- **Going-concern language in latest filing:** KPMG's audit opinion
  on the FY2025 10-K does not raise substantial doubt; however,
  structural cash burn ($143.9m FY2025 FCF deficit), $200m active
  ATM facility and EXIM covenant mean liquidity management remains
  critical.

## 11. Key risks

- **ATM equity dilution pace:** $200m open-market sales agreement
  active; historical issuance of $50–70m per quarter implies
  ~$200–280m annual dilution run-rate.
- **EXIM covenant:** $55m minimum cash covenant; Q1 FY2026
  unrestricted cash $311.8m provides 5.7x coverage but structural
  burn could tighten by H2 FY2026.
- **SOEC pre-commercial:** no commercial installations yet;
  Advanced Technologies backlog 1.6% of total and declining.
- **Data-centre pipeline conversion:** 1.5 GW+ proposals, 80%+
  unsigned; grid interconnection delays and customer capex
  constraints are the binding risks.
- **Opex reduction sustainability:** two restructurings already;
  third cut may be required if customer projects slip.
- **Gross margin recovery uncertainty:** FY2025 GM still −16.7%;
  requires 10–15% positive GM for sustainable profitability.
