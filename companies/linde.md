## 1. Business overview

Linde plc is the world's largest industrial gases company, operating
through Americas, EMEA and APAC segments and serving chemical,
refining, electronics, food & beverage, healthcare and metals
customers in 100+ countries. Clean hydrogen has become a strategic
growth pillar. Linde positions itself across the hydrogen value chain
as a producer (blue H2 via ATR + CCS; green H2 via PEM electrolysis),
distributor (pipelines, trucks, cryogenic tanks), and project /
engineering lead. Blue hydrogen dominates the near-term US portfolio;
green hydrogen is concentrated in North America (Niagara) and Europe
(Lingen / GET H2 Nukleus). Clean-energy project backlog disclosed at
USD 10bn+; hydrogen revenue growth target from ~USD 3bn today to
~USD 8bn under accelerated scenarios.

## 11. Key hydrogen risks

- **Long-term offtake execution:** hydrogen growth depends on
  multi-decade customer offtake (Beaumont, Fort Saskatchewan,
  Louisiana). ExxonMobil Baytown pause highlights policy-driven
  project risk.
- **US 45V uncertainty:** tightening emissions accounting, methane
  modelling and political turnover reduce visibility on blue-H2
  eligibility.
- **Capital concentration:** USD 10bn+ backlog implies ~USD 5–6bn of
  Linde-share capex over 5–8 years; cost overruns or schedule slips
  directly hit project IRR.
- **Blue H2 CCS dependency:** pipeline rights-of-way, injection
  permits and carbon-policy volatility constrain blue H2 economics.
- **Renewable power cost / availability:** green H2 economics depend
  on long-term PPA terms and grid connections.
- **Alternative decarbonisation pathways:** direct electrification,
  e-fuels and competing H2 technologies may erode demand.
