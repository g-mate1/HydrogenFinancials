**Headquarters:** Vienna, Austria
**Listing:** Vienna Stock Exchange, OMV

**Fiscal year end:** 31 December
**Segment:** Integrated energy company (upstream, refining at
Schwechat, chemicals via Borealis); hydrogen and SAF are central to
Strategy 2030 decarbonisation.
**Data last updated:** 2026-04-13
**Latest full fiscal year reported:** FY2024
**Latest quarter reported:** Q1 FY2025 (30 April 2025)

## 1. Business overview

OMV is Austria's largest integrated energy company, with upstream
operations in Central Europe and the Middle East, refining and
petrochemicals at Schwechat near Vienna, and majority ownership of
Borealis. Hydrogen and green fuels (SAF, e-methanol, chemical
recycling via ReOil®) anchor Strategy 2030's decarbonisation
roadmap, with Schwechat as the flagship site. OMV sits across
electrolyser operations (10 MW PEM live, 140 MW under construction),
industrial hydrogen demand (refining hydrotreatment, Borealis
petrochemical feedstock) and hydrogen import participation via the
SoutH2 Corridor.

## 5. Hydrogen-specific operational KPIs

- **Operational capacity:** 10 MW PEM electrolyser at Schwechat
  (UpHy Phase 1; live 2024–25), producing ~1,500 t H2/year.
- **Under construction:** 140 MW PEM electrolyser at Bruck an der
  Leitha (FID May 2025; Siemens Energy EPC; ~23,000 t H2/year from
  end-2027) — majority owned by OMV with Masdar 49% partner.
- **UpHy Phase 2 (55 MW total):** FID pending; ~8 kt H2/yr; €190m
  investment (€50m EU NRRP funding).
- **Funding secured:** €123m production funding from Austria
  Wirtschaftsservice (January 2026).
- **SAF roadmap:** scale from ~2,000 t (FY2024 pilot) to 700+ kt/year
  by 2030; co-processing plant at Schwechat operational 2024;
  Petrobrazi (Romania) 250 kt SAF/HVO FID taken June 2024 with
  two integrated green hydrogen plants.
- **SoutH2 Corridor participant** via Austria entry point.

## 11. Key hydrogen risks

- **Austrian hydrogen market scale:** limited domestic end-use
  outside refining and Borealis; SAF offtake depends on EU
  mandates.
- **Electricity supply for electrolysis:** 140 MW plant requires
  200+ MW of renewable power; Austrian grid and PPA constraints.
- **SAF offtake uncertainty:** voluntary corporate purchases
  insufficient; regulatory mandates must hold.
- **Borealis industrial H2 demand:** green H2 competes with cheaper
  grey/blue alternatives; ReOil® economics still unproven at 200 kt
  scale.
- **Refining margin trajectory:** European refining compressed by
  overcapacity; hydrogen capex raises fixed costs.
- **Hydrogen import competition via SoutH2:** domestic production
  economics vs imported H2.
