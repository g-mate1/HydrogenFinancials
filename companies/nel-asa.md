**Headquarters:** Oslo, Norway

**Segment:** Manufacturer of alkaline and PEM water electrolysers for

**Data last updated:** 2026-04-13
**Latest full fiscal year reported:** FY2024
**Latest quarter reported:** Q2 2025 (released ~June 2025)

## 1. Business overview

Nel ASA is a global manufacturer of alkaline and PEM water
electrolysers for green hydrogen production. Customers include
industrial end-users in ammonia, steel, petroleum refining and
chemicals across more than 80 countries. Nel operates a dual-
technology strategy: alkaline (lower cost, proven at scale; 1.0 GW at
Herøya) and PEM (higher efficiency, compact footprint; 0.5 GW at
Wallingford, Connecticut). Total nameplate manufacturing capacity is
1.5 GW/year. The company positions itself in hydrogen *production*,
supplying equipment and systems to project developers and industrial
operators. In June 2024 Nel completed a spin-off of its hydrogen
fuelling division (now Cavendish Hydrogen ASA), refocusing fully on
electrolyser manufacturing.

## 5. Hydrogen-specific operational KPIs

- **Installed / shipped capacity:** Not disclosed. Production
  capacity 1.5 GW/year (1.0 GW alkaline, 0.5 GW PEM). Wallingford PEM
  expansion (50 → 500 MW) approaching full ramp. Herøya temporarily
  curtailed from January 2025.
- **Order backlog:** NOK 1,249m end-Q2 2025 (−37.9% YoY, −14.4%
  QoQ). Alkaline ~NOK 1,000m; PEM ~NOK 249m.
- **Book-to-bill ratio (FY2024):** ~0.79x on implied annual intake of
  ~NOK 1,100m vs NOK 1,390m revenue.
- **Manufacturing capacity:** 1.5 GW/year nameplate; Herøya 1.0 GW
  alkaline (curtailed); Wallingford 0.5 GW PEM.
- **Announced large contracts (past 18 months):** Hy Stor Energy
  Mississippi (>1 GW alkaline, FEED Dec 2024); Reliance Industries
  (alkaline licensing for India, May 2024); GreenH (Slagen +
  Kristiansund); Fertiberia 20 MW PEM for Puertollano; Samsung E&A
  partnership (Q1 2025).

## 11. Key risks

1. **PEM profitability inflection delay:** PEM loss-making through
   FY2024 and Q1 2025 despite record Q1 order intake; Wallingford
   ramp delays would keep fixed cost high. Target: EBITDA breakeven
   Q4 2025–Q1 2026.
2. **Herøya halt / alkaline underutilisation:** Production suspended
   January 2025; Q1 alkaline EBITDA NOK (52m). Extension beyond Q3
   2025 would erode fixed-cost absorption further.
3. **EU Innovation Fund grant contingency:** EUR 135m award contingent
   on FID and procurement milestones; drawdown slippage into 2026–2027
   would strain cash.
4. **Customer & project concentration:** Hy Stor, Statkraft and
   Reliance licensing represent material shares of intake and backlog;
   Statkraft 40 MW cancellation in Q2 2025 exemplifies the risk.
5. **Hydrogen market FID slowdown:** Order intake −74% YoY in Q2
   2025 (NOK 71m); driven by slow 45V tax credit disbursements,
   elevated European power prices and policy uncertainty.
6. **Cost / competitive pressure:** Must prove cost reductions vs
   Plug Power, ITM/Linde, Siemens Energy and alkaline incumbents
   (thyssenkrupp nucera, John Cockerill) to sustain margin trajectory.
