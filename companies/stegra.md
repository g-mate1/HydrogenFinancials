## 1. Business overview

Stegra (rebranded from H2 Green Steel in September 2024) is a
private Swedish company building Europe's first large-scale
integrated green-steel plant at Boden, ~900 km north of
Stockholm. The facility combines a 740 MW alkaline electrolyser
(supplied by thyssenkrupp nucera in 37 modular 20 MW units) with
direct-reduced iron (DRI) production and electric-arc-furnace
(EAF) capacity to produce green steel at up to 95% lower CO2
intensity than traditional blast-furnace methods. Initial nameplate
capacity is 2.5 Mt/year green steel, expandable to 4.5 Mt/year. The
Boden site benefits from abundant Swedish renewable power (near
100% of grid supply). CEO: Henrik Henriksson (former Scania /
TRATON SE).

## 5. Hydrogen-specific operational KPIs

- **Electrolyser capacity:** 740 MW alkaline (37 × 20 MW modules
  from thyssenkrupp nucera). All modules installed by April 2026.
- **Order backlog:** ~50% of first-phase 2.5 Mt/year output
  committed via long-term binding offtake agreements.
- **Manufacturing capacity:** 2.5 Mt/year green steel initial;
  expandable to 4.5 Mt/year via DRI + EAF pathway. 740 MW
  electrolysis supports ~5 Mt/year DRI feedstock.
- **Offtake customers (past 12 months):** BMW (volume not
  disclosed); **Porsche 35 kt/yr**; **Mercedes-Benz ~50 kt/yr**
  across Europe and North America; Volvo Group / Volvo Cars
  (binding offtake + direct equity); Scania; ZF; IKEA; Electrolux;
  **Microsoft** (seven-year data-centre agreement, September 2025);
  Cargill.

## 6. Balance sheet and liquidity

- **Most recent capital raise:** March 2026 — >€2bn incremental
  equity + debt to offset cost overruns and complete construction.
  January 2024 initial project close: ~€2.1bn equity + ~€4.2bn
  green debt (~€6.5bn total project financing). 2025 raises
  included Hy24 (first new investor since 2024), Just Climate,
  Altor, FAM, Kallskär top-ups. Sweden granted SEK 390m (~€41m)
  public support (2024).
- **Cost overruns:** ~€1bn (~15% above initial budget) disclosed
  mid-2025 — infrastructure (railway, port) and capex inflation.
- **Cash runway:** Not disclosed; March 2026 raise required to
  close the financing gap and maintain the Q4 2026 commissioning
  target.

## 11. Key risks

- **Commissioning timeline risk:** Q4 2026 target already slipped
  ~3 months; further delays could trigger offtake renegotiation.
- **Cost-overrun risk:** ~€1bn overruns disclosed; March 2026
  raise barely covers the gap.
- **European steel-demand volatility** across automotive and
  construction cycles.
- **Electrolyser supply & scale-up** — thyssenkrupp nucera sole
  supplier of the 740 MW fleet.
- **Nordic power-price volatility** on ~6 TWh/yr electricity
  consumption.
- **Commodity price exposure** (iron ore, scrap, alloys).
- **Equity investor dilution & governance** complexity after
  multiple raises.
