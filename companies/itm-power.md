## 1. Business overview

ITM Power designs and manufactures large-scale PEM electrolysers for
industrial customers producing green hydrogen. The company is
headquartered in Sheffield, which houses the Bessemer Park
manufacturing facility — described as the world's largest electrolyser
factory — with current annual capacity of 1 GW. The product portfolio
includes the TRIDENT stack platform, POSEIDON (20 MW modules), and
the ALPHA 50 (50 MW integrated green hydrogen plant). A
next-generation platform, Chronos, targeting 2028 launch, aims to
reduce part count and double power density.

Core customers include Linde Engineering, RWE (Lingen 100 MW plants),
Yara (24 MW green ammonia facility in Norway), and Shell (REFHYNE II
100 MW reservation). ITM sits at the production node of the hydrogen
value chain. The company floated on AIM in 2004 and has received
strategic investments from Linde (£38m in 2019), Snam (£30m in 2020),
and in February 2026 a £40m UK government equity stake plus £46.5m
DESNZ grant for the Chronos manufacturing line.

## 5. Hydrogen-specific operational KPIs

- **Installed / shipped capacity:** 410 MW work-in-progress at end of
  FY2025; 40 MW revenue recognised. H1 FY2026: 50 TRIDENT skids and
  150 stacks delivered for Lingen One (100 MW plant, completed
  end-2025).
- **Order backlog:** £152m at 31 October 2025 (all contracted); 71%
  profitable (up from 60% in April 2025).
- **Book-to-bill ratio (H1 FY2026):** ~4.2x.
- **Manufacturing capacity:** Bessemer Park 1 GW/year; Chronos line
  targeted at 2028 commercial operation at 1 GW/year (£120m three-year
  investment).
- **Announced large contracts (past 12 months):** Lingen One & Two
  (RWE / Linde, 200 MW total); Shell REFHYNE II 100 MW reservation;
  Linde GET H2 Nukleus 200 MW; ALPHA 50 standardised 50 MW product
  launched October 2025 at €50m full-scope price.

## 11. Key risks

- **Chronos execution and technology risk (2028 launch target):**
  £120m three-year capex programme includes a new autostacker robot
  and compressed stack design. Slippage beyond 2028, cost overruns or
  performance shortfalls would delay gross-margin recovery.
- **UK government shareholder governance risk:** GBE's 10.8% stake
  plus Linde (~20%) creates multi-stakeholder governance; policy
  shifts could constrain export or technology-transfer decisions.
- **Backlog margin quality:** 29% of backlog remains loss-making;
  H1 FY2026 gross margin of −36.1% illustrates the drag.
- **Lingen Two delivery risk:** 100 MW RWE / Linde second unit
  critical to FY2027 visibility; slippage would cascade.
- **Hydrogen-market demand volatility:** backlog and proposal
  pipeline depend on customer capex and sustained policy support;
  competitive intensity rising (Plug Power, Nel, Siemens Energy).
- **Cash burn and working-capital timing:** despite £197.8m cash and
  zero debt, upfront Bessemer Park expansion and Chronos tooling
  spending tighten the runway if revenues slip.
