**Latest quarter reported:** H1 FY2026 (six months ended 31 October

## 1. Business overview

ITM Power designs and manufactures large-scale PEM electrolysers for
industrial customers producing green hydrogen. The company is
headquartered in Sheffield, which houses the Bessemer Park
manufacturing facility — described as the world's largest electrolyser
factory — with a current annual capacity of 1 GW. The product portfolio
includes the TRIDENT stack platform, POSEIDON (20 MW modules), and the
ALPHA 50 (50 MW integrated green hydrogen plant). A next-generation
platform, Chronos, targeting 2028 launch, will feature reduced part
count and doubled power density.

Core customers include Linde Engineering, RWE (Lingen 100 MW plants),
Yara (24 MW green ammonia facility in Norway), and Shell (REFHYNE II
100 MW reservation). ITM sits at the production node of the hydrogen
value chain — manufacturing the core electrolyser technology that
customers integrate into broader hydrogen infrastructure.

ITM Power floated on London's AIM Market in 2004 and has received
strategic investments from Linde (£38m in 2019), Snam (£30m in 2020),
and in February 2026 a £40m UK government equity stake plus £46.5m
DESNZ grant to support the Chronos manufacturing line.

## 5. Hydrogen-specific operational KPIs

- **Installed / shipped capacity:** 410 MW work-in-progress at end of
  FY2025; 40 MW revenue recognised. H1 FY2026: 50 TRIDENT skids and 150
  stacks delivered for Lingen One (100 MW plant, completed end-2025).
- **Order backlog:** £152m as of 31 October 2025 (all contracted, no
  merchant conditions); 71% from profitable contracts (up from 60% in
  April 2025).
- **Book-to-bill ratio (FY2026, H1):** Approximately 4.0x.
- **Manufacturing capacity:** Bessemer Park 1 GW/year. Chronos
  manufacturing line (£120m investment over three years) targeted at
  commercial operation 2028 with 1 GW annual capacity.
- **Announced large contracts (past 12 months):** Lingen One & Two (RWE
  / Linde, 200 MW total; Lingen One completed end-2025); Shell REFHYNE
  II 100 MW reservation; Linde GET H2 Nukleus 200 MW; ALPHA 50
  standardised 50 MW product launched October 2025 at €50m full-scope
  price.

## 6. Balance sheet and liquidity
