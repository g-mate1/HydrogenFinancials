## 1. Business overview

Ballard Power Systems designs, develops and manufactures fuel cell
modules for zero-emission applications in heavy-duty transportation
and power generation. The primary product line is the FCmove® family
of fuel cell power modules (120–360 kW scalable), which power
hydrogen-fuelled buses, commercial trucks, trains and marine vessels.
The company has delivered nearly 4,000 units across OEM partners
including New Flyer, Wrightbus, Solaris and Van Hool, accumulating
more than 150 million kilometres of operational experience.

Manufacturing is centred in Burnaby, British Columbia, with three
facilities (240,000+ sq ft) and nameplate capacity of over 1 GW/year.
Ballard sits at the fuel-cell end-use segment of the hydrogen value
chain. The 9th-generation FCmove-XD launched in May 2024 (0.36 kW/L,
30,000+ hour design life). In March 2026 Ballard announced a
commercial agreement with New Flyer for 50 MW of fuel cell bus
engines, with deliveries beginning in 2026.

## 11. Key risks

1. **Order-book contraction:** 12-month order intake −73% YoY in Q4;
   total backlog −31% YoY. Successful conversion of the $53.9m
   12-month orderbook is critical for 2026 guidance.
   [Q4 2025 release](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/ballard-reports-q4-2025-and-full-year-results-302711955.html)
2. **China exit / Weichai JV uncertainty:** Exit from the world's
   largest hydrogen-vehicle market removes anticipated revenue and
   raises the possibility of asset write-downs.
3. **Texas Gigafactory cancellation:** Q3 2025 decision to cancel the
   planned 3 GW facility, citing evolving U.S. subsidy conditions,
   leaves the company exposed to policy shifts.
4. **Hydrogen refuelling infrastructure dependency:** Ballard's growth
   depends on third-party development of hydrogen distribution and
   refuelling networks that it does not control.
5. **New Flyer execution risk:** 50 MW / 500-unit commercial agreement
   is the largest customer commitment; missed delivery milestones
   would materially impact 2026/2027 revenue.
6. **Rail & marine segment fragility:** +862% rail growth is off a
   small base; early-adoption segments are exposed to project
   deferrals and technology shifts.
