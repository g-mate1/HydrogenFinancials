**Data last updated:** 2026-04-13

**Latest quarter reported:** Q4 FY2025, 12 March 2026

## 1. Business overview

Ballard Power Systems designs, develops and manufactures fuel cell
modules for zero-emission applications in the heavy-duty transportation
and power generation sectors. The primary product line is the FCmove®
family of fuel cell power modules (120–360 kW scalable), which power
hydrogen-fuelled buses, commercial trucks, trains and marine vessels.
The company has delivered nearly 4,000 units across OEM partners
including New Flyer (North America), Wrightbus (UK/Europe), Solaris and
Van Hool, accumulating more than 150 million kilometres of operational
experience.

Manufacturing is centred in Burnaby, British Columbia, with three
facilities (240,000+ sq ft) and capacity to produce over 1 GW of PEM
fuel cell stacks and modules annually. The company operates additional
engineering and sales offices in North America and Europe. Ballard sits
at the fuel cell end-use segment of the hydrogen value chain, converting
hydrogen into electricity for propulsion and backup power.

The 9th generation module, FCmove-XD, launched May 2024 (0.36 kW/L
volumetric density, 30,000+ hour design life). In March 2026 management
announced a commercial agreement with New Flyer for 50 MW of fuel cell
bus engines, deliveries commencing 2026.

## 5. Hydrogen-specific operational KPIs

- **Installed / shipped capacity:** Nearly 800 fuel cell engines shipped
  in FY2025; cumulative 4,000+ units operating worldwide with 150+
  million kilometres logged.
- **Order backlog:** $119.3m total backlog; $53.9m 12-month orderbook
  as of 31 December 2025 (down from $173.5m / $98.9m at end-2024).
- **Book-to-bill ratio (FY2025):** ~0.54.
- **Manufacturing capacity:** 1+ GW/year nameplate across three
  Canadian facilities; currently operating well below nameplate.
- **Announced large contracts (past 12 months):** New Flyer commercial
  agreement for 50 MW of fuel cell bus engines (March 2026, deliveries
  from 2026); eCap & Samskip marine order ($19.1m, Q3 2025).

## 6. Balance sheet and liquidity

- **Cash runway (months):** ~97 months at FY2025 burn rate; Q4 2025
  delivered positive operating cash flow of $11.4m (strongest quarter in
  a decade), suggesting improving trajectory.
- **Most recent capital raise:** None disclosed in past 12 months.
- **Convertible / debt instruments outstanding:** $23.3m total debt
  (capital lease obligations); no convertible notes or bank debt.
- **Going-concern language in latest filing:** No going-concern
  commentary; management emphasises strong cash, no bank debt, and
  "no near- or mid-term financing requirements".

## 7. Notable recent events (past 12 months)

- **Restructuring and cost reduction:** Global restructuring from Q3
  2024 delivered 30%+ reduction in operating expenses and 32% reduction
  in cash operating costs; headcount 1,173 → 887 → ~814–949.
- **Profitability inflection:** FY2025 net loss $90.9m (vs $323.5m);
  gross margin +37pp to 5%; positive Q4 OCF $11.4m.
- **Product launches:** FCmove-XD (9th gen, May 2024); FCmove-SC
  scalable compact variant (Q3 2025).
- **Strategic exits:** Halted further China investment; cancelled
  planned Texas Gigafactory (Q3 2025) citing U.S. hydrogen funding
  delays.
- **Major orders:** 50 MW New Flyer commercial agreement; marine
  orders; bus segment revenue grew 51% in FY2024.
- **Q4 2025 operational improvements:** Cash opex −41% YoY; gross
  margin 17%; 12-month orderbook $53.9m provides 2026 visibility.
