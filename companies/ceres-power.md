## 1. Business overview

Ceres Power Holdings plc is a UK developer and licensor of proprietary
solid oxide fuel cell and electrolyser stack technology for
decentralised stationary power and green hydrogen production. The
company operates an asset-light licensing business model, licensing
its ceramic stack technology to industrial partners for manufacture
under licence. Ceres does not manufacture equipment directly; it
receives licence fees, milestone payments, and ongoing royalties as
licensees scale production.

Addressable markets include power generation for industrial facilities,
commercial buildings, data centres (notably AI) and renewable hydrogen
production via electrolysis. Licensing partners have included Bosch
(terminated Feb 2025), Doosan (South Korea), Delta Electronics
(Taiwan), Denso (Japan), Weichai Power (China), Shell and Thermax
(India). Ceres is positioned in the end-use segment of the hydrogen
value chain for stationary power; licensees handle manufacturing and
integration.

The business model has transitioned during 2024–2025 from one-off
technology transfer fees to recurring royalty revenues, with first
royalties from Doosan commencing late 2025. Gross margin remains high
(77% FY2024; 70% FY2025) reflecting the capital-light nature of
licensing, though operating losses continue as R&D investment stays
elevated.

## 5. Hydrogen-specific operational KPIs

- **Installed / shipped capacity:** Doosan commenced 50 MW annual mass
  production in South Korea mid-2025. Shell deployed a 1 MW SOEC system
  in Bangalore, India with Thermax. FY2024 order intake £112.8m.
- **Order backlog:** £45m run-rate disclosed for 2026, with further
  Weichai, Doosan, Delta, Denso and Thermax deployments expected.
- **Book-to-bill ratio (FY2025):** ~1.38 (implied £45m 2026 visibility
  / £32.6m FY2025 revenue).
- **Manufacturing capacity:** Not independently verified; Ceres does
  not own manufacturing facilities — Doosan 50 MW/year.
- **Announced large contracts (past 12 months):** Weichai Power
  manufacturing licence (5 Nov 2025) for SOFC stationary power
  targeting AI data centres; Centrica multi-gigawatt strategic
  partnership (27 Mar 2026) for UK and Europe; Doosan mass production
  commenced Q3 2025 at 50 MW/year.

## 6. Balance sheet and liquidity

- **Cash runway (months):** ~51 months at FY2025 quarterly burn rate.
  £83.3m cash provides substantial runway even if burn persists.
- **Most recent capital raise:** None since FY2024. Ceres maintains
  zero debt and relies on cash reserves and future royalties.
- **Convertible / debt instruments outstanding:** None.
- **Going-concern language in latest filing:** No going-concern warning
  for Ceres Power itself. (Commentary did address associate company
  RFC Power Limited.) Transition from upfront fees to royalty streams
  carries execution risk.

## 7. Notable recent events (past 12 months)

- **Bosch partnership termination (20 Feb 2025):** Major strategic blow
  — Bosch discontinued SOFC system development and divested its 17.4%
  stake. Share price fell 35–42% on the day. Bosch board
  representative resigned.
- **FY2024 record results (21 Mar 2025):** Revenue £51.9m (+132% YoY)
  driven by Delta (£20.2m upfront, £22m deferred), Denso, Thermax
  licence deals. Gross margin 77%; adjusted EBITDA loss narrowed from
  (£50.3m) to (£22.3m).
- **FY2025 revenue decline (26 Mar 2026):** Revenue −37% to £32.6m as
  company transitioned from technology-transfer fees to recurring
  royalties (Delta deferred payment £22m did not repeat). Gross margin
  held at 70%. Cash burn improved £35.9m → £19.2m.
- **Business Transformation Plan (Sept 2025):** Cost reduction
  programme targeting 20% opex cut in 2026; headcount 478 → ~440.
- **Doosan mass production commencement (Jul 2025):** 50 MW/year
  stationary power production in South Korea; first royalty revenue
  from Doosan began flowing Q4 2025.
- **Weichai manufacturing licence (5 Nov 2025):** SOFC stationary power
  for Chinese market.
- **Centrica partnership (27 Mar 2026):** Multi-gigawatt UK/Europe
  SOFC deployment.
- **2026 revenue guidance:** ~£45m before new business, implying
  recovery driven by Doosan, Delta, Denso manufacturing ramp-ups.
