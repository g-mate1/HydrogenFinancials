## 1. Business overview

Ceres Power Holdings plc is a UK developer and licensor of
proprietary solid oxide fuel cell and electrolyser stack technology
for decentralised stationary power and green hydrogen production.
Ceres operates an asset-light licensing business model: it licenses
ceramic stack technology to industrial partners for manufacture under
licence, receiving licence fees, milestone payments and ongoing
royalties as licensees scale production. Addressable markets include
industrial stationary power, commercial buildings, data centres
(notably AI) and renewable hydrogen. Licensees include Doosan (South
Korea), Delta Electronics (Taiwan), Denso (Japan), Weichai Power
(China), Shell, Thermax (India) and the new Centrica partnership;
Bosch's partnership was terminated in February 2025.

The business model transitioned during 2024–2025 from one-off
technology-transfer fees to recurring royalty revenues, with first
royalties from Doosan commencing in late 2025. Gross margin remains
high (77% FY2024, 70% FY2025) reflecting the capital-light nature of
licensing, though operating losses persist.

- **Installed / shipped capacity:** Doosan commenced 50 MW annual
  mass production in South Korea mid-2025. Shell deployed a 1 MW

- **Order backlog:** £45m run-rate disclosed for 2026.
- **Book-to-bill ratio (FY2025):** ~1.38.
- **Manufacturing capacity:** Not applicable — Ceres does not own

- **Announced large contracts (past 12 months):** Weichai licence
  (5 Nov 2025); Centrica strategic partnership (27 Mar 2026);
  Doosan mass production commenced Q3 2025.

## 11. Key risks

- **Royalty ramp timing & execution** — material streams expected
  only from 2027 onwards.
- **Bosch aftermath on stack economics** — loss of key validation
  partner and board oversight.
- **China IP transfer risk (Weichai)** — staged IP transfer of up
  to £30m; geopolitical and enforcement risk.
- **Licensing model fragility** — 7–8 licensees; loss of any major
  partner would materially impact 2026–2027 guidance.
- **Cash burn persistence** — FY2025 operating loss £32.5m; 20%
  opex cut may not achieve breakeven without faster royalty ramp.
- **Technology / competitive risk** — SOFC stack economics still
  unproven at scale vs PEM electrolysers, battery + turbine hybrids,
  and conventional fuel cells.
