## 1. Business overview

BP plc is a globally integrated energy company spanning upstream,
downstream, gas & low-carbon energy, and customers & products,
operating in ~70 countries. Hydrogen activity sits within the
low-carbon energy segment and is primarily targeted at decarbonising
existing refineries and adjacent industrial hubs (Germany, Spain,
UK, Australia). Following CEO Murray Auchincloss's "resetting the
strategy" announcement in February 2025, BP has pared its hydrogen
pipeline to 5–7 high-graded projects globally by 2030, cancelling
or pausing 18–24 earlier-stage initiatives, and sharply cutting
annual low-carbon capex to $0.8–2.0bn.

## 5. Hydrogen-specific operational KPIs

- **Committed capacity (active):** Lingen (Germany) 100 MW green H2
  (FID Dec 2024, COD 2027, Accelera electrolyser); Castellón (Spain)
  25 MW green H2 50/50 JV with Iberdrola (COD H2 2026, ~2,800 t/yr
  to BP Castellón refinery).
- **Cancelled / withdrawn (past 12 months):** HyGreen Teesside (500
  MW green) cancelled March 2025; H2Teesside (1.2 GW blue) withdrawn
  December 2025; Kwinana (Australia, ~100+ MW green + RFuel) paused
  2024; AREH Pilbara (40 GW+) exited July 2025; 18–24 early-stage
  projects halted in 2024–25 (saving ~$200m/year).
- **Order backlog / book-to-bill / manufacturing:** not applicable
  (BP is an owner-operator).

- **Feb 2025 Strategy Reset:** Upstream capex raised to $10bn by
  2027; low-carbon capex cut to $1.5–2.0bn/year, down to
  $0.8bn/year by 2027; 5–7 hydrogen & CCS projects retained for the
  decade.
- **Oct 2024:** 18 early-stage hydrogen projects halted (saving
  ~$200m/year).
- **Dec 2024:** Lingen 100 MW FID taken (Accelera 100 MW
  electrolyser contracted).
- **Feb 2025:** Construction started on Castellón 25 MW (50/50 JV

- **Dec 2025:** H2Teesside blue hydrogen (1.2 GW, UK) withdrawn from

- **April 2025:** Q1 2025 profit $0.7bn; net debt $27.0bn.

## 11. Key hydrogen risks

- **Strategy reset & capex reallocation risk:** low-carbon capex
  cut to $0.8–2.0bn/year; further H2 deferrals possible.
- **Execution risk on remaining projects:** Lingen and Castellón
  are still early-stage; construction delays or offtake shortfalls
  could trigger additional de-prioritisation.
- **Demand & offtake exposure:** both active projects rely on
  internal (refinery) decarbonisation offtake, with limited
  third-party demand secured.
- **UK / EU policy & subsidy headwinds:** H2Teesside withdrawal
  illustrates land-use competition and demand weakness.
- **US 45V eligibility:** not applicable to BP's European focus,
  but any future US pivot faces tightening rules.
- **Impairment risk:** 18–24 paused/cancelled projects create
  significant write-down exposure.
