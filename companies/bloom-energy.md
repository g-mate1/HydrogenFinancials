**Reporting currency:** USD
**Fiscal year end:** 31 December
**Segment:** Stationary fuel cell (SOFC) and electrolyser (SOEC) power
generation for industrial, commercial, and data centre customers.
**Data last updated:** 2026-04-13
**Latest full fiscal year reported:** FY2024
**Latest quarter reported:** Q1 FY2025, 30 April 2025

## 1. Business overview

Bloom Energy Corporation manufactures solid-oxide fuel cell (SOFC)
systems for on-site stationary power generation and, increasingly,
solid-oxide electrolyser (SOEC) products for hydrogen production. The
primary revenue driver remains the Bloom Energy Server, a containerised
SOFC platform deployed at commercial, industrial, utility, and
hyperscale data centre sites across North America and internationally.
The company has shipped 1.2+ GW cumulative SOFC capacity since
inception, with strong growth driven by data centre and AI
infrastructure demand. Bloom also manufactures hydrogen electrolysers
in partnership with companies like Shell, but SOEC revenue
contribution remains immaterial relative to SOFC. Manufacturing
facilities in Delaware and California; the company plans to expand to
2 GW annual production capacity by end-2026.

## 5. Hydrogen-specific operational KPIs

- **Installed / shipped capacity:** 1.2+ GW SOFC cumulative deployed
  through FY2024; targeting 2 GW annual nameplate capacity by end-2026.
- **Order backlog:** $2.5bn product backlog (end FY2024); ~$6bn product
  + $14bn service backlog (≈ $20bn total) reported at end FY2025 (Feb
  2026 disclosure).
- **Book-to-bill ratio (FY2024):** $2.5bn / $1.47bn ≈ 1.7x.
- **Manufacturing capacity:** 1 GW current (FY2024); expansion to 2 GW
  underway.
- **Announced large contracts (past 12 months):** AEP subsidiary deal
  (Nov 2024) 100 MW + 900 MW option ($2.65bn exercised Jan 2025);
  Brookfield $5bn AI infrastructure partnership (Oct 2025); Oracle
  hyperscaler deal (July 2025); Equinix data centre deployments (100+
  MW across 19 sites).

## 6. Balance sheet and liquidity

- **Cash runway (months):** Very long. At Q1 2025 operating loss of
  ~$19m and cash ~$900m, runway 48+ months; company now generating
  positive operating cash flow.
- **Most recent capital raise:** None disclosed FY2024–Q1 2025; FY2024
  was first year of positive operating cash flow.
- **Convertible / debt instruments outstanding:** $1.388bn total debt
  (mix of convertible notes and senior secured debt); breakdown not
  disclosed in recent releases.
- **Going-concern language in latest filing:** None; no going-concern
  warning.

## 7. Notable recent events (past 12 months)

- **AI data centre demand surge:** Hyperscale bookings (Oracle,
  Equinix, Brookfield, AEP) now dominate new-order intake.
- **Record backlog growth:** Product backlog to $6bn at end FY2025
  (Feb 2026 disclosure), +140% YoY.
- **Manufacturing capacity expansion:** ~$100m to scale from 1 GW to
  2 GW by end-2026.
- **Margin recovery:** Gross margin 14.8% (FY2023) → 27.5% (FY2024)
  → 29% non-GAAP Q1 FY2025.
- **First full year of operating income:** $22.9m EBIT (FY2024) after
  two years of operating losses.
- **Net loss narrowed:** $307.9m → $27.2m.
- **CFO transition:** Dan Berenbaum departed 1 May 2025; interim
  coverage by Chief Accounting Officer pending permanent hire.
- **Headcount:** −10.5% YoY (FY2024) reflecting efficiency focus.
