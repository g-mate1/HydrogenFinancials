## 1. Business overview

Bloom Energy Corporation manufactures solid-oxide fuel cell (SOFC)
systems for on-site stationary power generation and, increasingly,
solid-oxide electrolyser (SOEC) products for hydrogen production.
The primary revenue driver is the Bloom Energy Server, a
containerised SOFC platform deployed at commercial, industrial,
utility and hyperscale data-centre sites across North America and
internationally. Bloom has shipped 1.2+ GW cumulative SOFC capacity
since inception, with recent growth driven overwhelmingly by data
centre and AI infrastructure demand. Manufacturing facilities sit in
Delaware and California; nameplate capacity is being expanded to
2 GW/year by end-2026.

## 5. Hydrogen-specific operational KPIs

- **Installed / shipped capacity:** 1.2+ GW SOFC cumulative through
  FY2025; targeting 2 GW annual nameplate capacity by end-2026.
- **Order backlog:** $6.0bn product backlog at end FY2025 (+140%
  YoY); ~$20bn total including service.
- **Book-to-bill ratio (FY2025):** ~3.0x.
- **Manufacturing capacity:** 1 GW current; expansion to 2 GW in
  progress.
- **Announced large contracts (past 12 months):** AEP subsidiary deal
  (Nov 2024) 100 MW + 900 MW option ($2.65bn exercised Jan 2025);
  Brookfield $5bn AI infrastructure partnership (Oct 2025) with
  European deployment Q1 2026; Oracle hyperscaler (Jul 2025); Equinix
  (100+ MW across 19 sites); CoreWeave.

## 6. Balance sheet and liquidity

## 11. Key risks

1. **Hyperscaler customer concentration:** 6–8 hyperscale customers
   drive the bulk of new orders; single-customer order loss would
   hit quarterly results.
2. **SOEC immaturity:** hydrogen electrolyser business remains
   early-stage and immaterial to revenue.
3. **Debt load & capital intensity:** total debt $3.62bn; $2.2bn
   convertibles require eventual refinancing or equity conversion.
4. **CFO transition & execution risk:** new CFO onboarding during a
   high-growth ramp.
5. **Valuation & dilution:** EV/Sales ~23x; further dilution likely
   as convertibles convert.
6. **Backlog conversion:** $6bn product backlog depends on timely
   Fremont/Delaware expansion, component supply and labour.
