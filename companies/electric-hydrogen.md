## 1. Business overview

Electric Hydrogen Co. (EH2) is a US private company developing
modular, high-efficiency 100 MW PEM electrolyser plants designed for
scalable green hydrogen production at industrial sites and
renewables-rich hubs. Founded in 2020 by former Tesla, First Solar
and Bloom Energy executives, the company positions itself in
hydrogen production (electrolyser hardware and integrated plant
systems) and targets customers needing multi-GW-scale hydrogen
output — industrial decarbonisation, green ammonia, e-fuels, and
more recently AI / data-centre power. EH2 has commissioned a stack
and systems manufacturing facility in Devens, Massachusetts
(operational 2024), unveiled its HyPrime 100 platform, and has
publicly named NextEra Energy, Galp, AES and (from 2025) Amazon as
customers or partners. Targeted manufacturing capacity is ~1.2
GW/year by the late 2020s.

## 10. Technology & customer portfolio

- **Product:** HyPrime 100 — 100 MW integrated PEM electrolyser
  plant, reportedly achieving best-in-class electrical efficiency
  (targeted <48 kWh/kg H2 at cell level) and higher current density
  than incumbent PEM designs.
- **Manufacturing:** Devens, MA stack and systems plant; target 1.2
  GW/year nameplate late 2020s.
- **Named customers / partners:** NextEra Energy (USA), Galp
  (Portugal refinery / Sines Hub), AES (USA), Amazon (USA, 2025),
  Breakthrough Energy Ventures (strategic partner).
- **Technology differentiators (claimed):** high current density
  (≥3 A/cm²), modular 100 MW block, shorter construction cycle,
  improved stack lifetime.

## 11. Key risks

- **Private-market funding cliff:** USD 380m Series C (Oct 2023)
  runway will eventually require a Series D or bridge; next raise
  may be materially harder in the 2025–26 hydrogen funding
  environment given McPhy liquidation, Everfuel privatisation, and
  BP/Shell strategy pullbacks.
- **Customer order conversion:** announced partnerships (Amazon,
  Galp, NextEra, AES) are not disclosed as firm purchase orders;
  risk of slippage or renegotiation.
- **Competitive intensity:** PEM incumbents (Plug Power, ITM Power
  / Linde, Siemens Energy, Cummins/Accelera) are scaling
  manufacturing at sub-EUR 700/kW targets — EH2 must prove
  differentiated LCOH economics to win at scale.
- **Technology commercialisation risk:** HyPrime 100 stack
  durability, stack lifetime and field performance at scale are
  still being validated.
- **Supply-chain dependencies:** iridium and PFAS-based membrane
  inputs face supply and regulatory pressure in Europe.
- **Founder / key-person risk:** small founding team from Tesla /
  First Solar / Bloom; departures would dent investor confidence.
