## 1. Business overview

Hexagon Purus is a global Type 4 composite pressure-vessel specialist
spun off from Hexagon Composites ASA in 2021. It offers Type 4 high-
pressure hydrogen storage cylinders and integrated fuel-storage
systems for zero-emission commercial vehicles, transit buses,
distribution trailers and stationary applications. Customers
include Hyundai, Hyzon, Solaris, CaetanoBus, New Flyer, Hino Motors
and, historically, Nikola (Chapter 11 in Q1 2025). Primary revenue
streams span hydrogen mobility (cylinders and fuel-cell system
integration), hydrogen infrastructure and distribution (high-pressure
trailers, dispensing equipment) and a legacy battery-systems
business. Manufacturing spans Norway (Lysaker, Ålesund), Germany
(Kassel, Weeze), the USA (Westminster MD, Dallas TX) and Canada
(Kelowna battery facility) with ~430 FTE post-2025 restructuring.

## 10. Segment / revenue mix

- **Hydrogen Mobility & Infrastructure (HMI):** ~98% of revenue
  (~NOK 1,121m in FY2025 est.), split between:
  - **Hydrogen Infrastructure** (trailers, refuelling, storage):
    ~60% of HMI.
  - **Hydrogen Mobility** (Type 4 cylinders, integrated fuel-cell
    systems for buses and trucks): ~29% of HMI; strong transit-bus
    demand (Solaris, CaetanoBus, New Flyer) but depressed heavy-duty
    truck contribution post-Nikola.
- **Battery Systems & Vehicle Integration (BVI):** ~2% of revenue
  (~NOK 23m in FY2025 est.); legacy battery packs (Toyota North
  America, Hino); Panasonic Energy cell supply partnership at
  Kelowna.

## 11. Key risks

- **Customer concentration & Nikola exposure:** heavy reliance on
  transit-bus OEMs and residual Nikola receivables risk.
- **Heavy-duty truck H2 adoption delays:** OEM launches pushed to
  end of decade; US policy uncertainty post-2024 election.
- **Capex intensity & cash runway:** 2023–24 expansion left the
  company cash-tight; convertible bond maturity in Dec 2028 looms
  with conversion price (NOK 12.61) far above current trading.
- **Currency risk:** NOK reporting vs EUR / USD cost base.
- **Battery-segment legacy exposure:** competition from Chinese LFP
  producers; Panasonic dependency.
- **Market-visibility risk:** slower-than-expected hydrogen project
  realisation in Europe and North America.

## 12. Upcoming catalysts

- **Nikola bankruptcy outcome:** potential recovery or write-off
  clarification.
- **Transit-bus order acceleration** (New Flyer, Solaris, CaetanoBus
  volume ramps in 2026–27).
- **Hydrogen distribution scaling:** EUR 6.2m distribution order
  (2026 delivery); potential follow-ons with European utilities.
- **Profitability inflection:** break-even contingent on sustained
  order intake; management guides to H2 2026 cash improvement.
- **Convertible refinance / equity restructuring** ahead of 2028
  maturity.
- **US hydrogen policy clarity:** unlock of heavy-duty truck
  demand.
