**Headquarters:** Perth, Western Australia

**Reporting currency:** AUD
**Fiscal year end:** 30 June
**Segment:** Diversified mining and energy — iron ore production

**Data last updated:** 2026-04-13
**Latest full fiscal year reported:** FY2025 (30 June 2025)
**Latest quarter reported:** H1 FY2026 (six months ended 31 Dec 2025)

## 1. Business overview

Fortescue Ltd (formerly Fortescue Metals Group) is Australia's
fourth-largest iron ore producer. In FY2025 it shipped a record
198.4 Mt of iron ore generating ~AUD 15.5bn revenue. In 2021 the
company launched Fortescue Future Industries (FFI); FFI was
integrated into the parent in July 2023 and the group was rebranded
to Fortescue Ltd to support a diversified energy strategy. In July
2024 Fortescue announced a major restructuring / de-scoping of its
earlier 15 Mt/year green hydrogen target, cutting ~700 jobs and
deferring flagship projects. In **July 2025 the company cancelled
the PEM50 platform and the 80 MW Phoenix Hydrogen Hub (Arizona)**
and took a USD 150m pre-tax impairment, citing US subsidy
uncertainty and non-competitive economics. Remaining hydrogen
focus is on Pecém (Brazil), Holmaneset (Norway), green-iron R&D
(Christmas Creek pilot 2025), and Pilbara operations'
decarbonisation (Real Zero by 2030).

## 5. Hydrogen-specific operational KPIs

- **Gladstone electrolyser factory:** 2 GW nameplate; operational
  April 2024 but currently in R&D mode following PEM50
  cancellation; ~30 staff cuts in May 2025.
- **Cancelled / paused projects:** PEM50 (50 MW Gladstone,
  cancelled July 2025); Phoenix Hub Arizona 80 MW (cancelled July
  2025); Gibson Island 550 MW (cancelled November 2024, land
  sold). USD 150m pre-tax impairment July 2025.
- **Active pipeline:** Pecém (Brazil) 168 kt H2/yr + 946 kt
  ammonia/yr with Petrobras — government-approved October 2024,
  USD ~5bn capex, FID pending; Holmaneset (Norway) 300 MW green
  hub with Statkraft PPA; TES (Germany) Wilhelmshaven supply
  partnership deferred to 2026.
- **Green iron pilot:** Christmas Creek 1,500 t/yr launched 2025.

## 6. Balance sheet and liquidity

## 11. Key hydrogen risks

- **Project cancellation escalation:** USD 150m July 2025 write-
  down signals vulnerability; Pecém and Holmaneset FIDs still at
  risk.
- **Commodity H2 market weakness:** sub-USD 5/kg projects
  uncompetitive with fossil pathways.
- **Electricity cost / subsidy policy:** US 45V uncertainty, Nordic
  power prices.
- **Gladstone factory underutilisation** and potential closure.
- **Andrew Forrest founder-led strategy volatility.**
- **15 Mt/yr target walk-back** has damaged credibility.
- **Iron ore price downside** would force capex retrenchment.
