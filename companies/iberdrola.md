## 1. Business overview

Iberdrola is one of the world's largest renewable power generators
and clean-energy utilities, with operations in Spain, the UK, the
US, Brazil and Mexico. The three core business lines are electricity
networks (distribution and transmission), renewable generation and
energy management. The US and UK together represent ~70% of capital
deployment. In green hydrogen, Iberdrola positions itself as a
producer: it develops electrolysis capacity powered by its own
renewable generation and couples production to large industrial
offtakers. Flagship: Puertollano 20 MW PEM plant (operational 2022,
3,000 t/yr, Fertiberia offtake); 800 MW portfolio across
Puertollano and Palos de la Frontera phases through 2027 (€1.8bn);
Castellón 25 MW with BP (H2 2026). Partners include Fertiberia, BP,
Cepsa, Total, ArcelorMittal.

## 5. Hydrogen-specific operational KPIs

- **Operating / committed capacity:** Puertollano 20 MW PEM (3,000
  t/yr, 2022); Castellón 25 MW with BP (2,800 t/yr, COD H2 2026);
  Palos de la Frontera 200 MW under construction; total ~45 MW
  operating + under construction, with a portfolio target of 800
  MW by 2027.
- **Capex commitment:** ~€1.8bn across 800 MW phased portfolio.
- **Customer offtake:** Fertiberia (primary; green ammonia);
  BP Castellón refinery; Cepsa, Total, ArcelorMittal partnerships
  under evaluation.
- **EU Hydrogen Bank (May 2024):** Iberdrola projects received
  material share of €100m+ in support among 132 European bids.

## 11. Key hydrogen risks

- **Electricity price volatility** for electrolysis (current green
  vs grey H2 gap €4–6/kg).
- **Customer offtake certainty:** revenue concentration on
  Fertiberia and BP; broader merchant H2 still immature.
- **EU Hydrogen Bank pricing compression** as more projects qualify.
- **Import competition** from North Africa, Australia, Middle East.
- **Electrolyser supply-chain capex** stickiness limits 800 MW ramp.
- **Regulatory shifts** (CBAM, RFNBO definitions, grid-connection
  timing).
