## 1. Business overview

Shell operates a global hydrogen business within its Renewables &
Energy Solutions (RES) segment, focused increasingly on industrial-
scale green hydrogen production and own-use refinery decarbonisation.
In 2024–2025 Shell decisively shifted away from consumer fuel-cell
mobility (closing all light-duty California stations in February 2025)
toward heavy-duty industrial electrolysis, concentrated in Northern
Europe (Netherlands, Germany) and the Middle East (Oman). Key
operating assets are REFHYNE I (10 MW PEM at Wesseling, Germany,
since 2021) and a 20 MW unit in China. Projects under construction
include Holland Hydrogen 1 (200 MW, Port of Rotterdam, COD H2 2025)
and REFHYNE II (100 MW, FID 2024, operations 2027). A 35% equity
stake in the 1.8 Mt/year Green Energy Oman project was confirmed in
early 2025 with first-phase production targeted for 2027.

## 11. Key hydrogen risks

- **Strategy pullback & industrial focus:** Decisive exit from
  consumer H2 mobility removes an addressable market but also
  signals weaker confidence in near-term H2 demand growth.
- **HH1 execution and cost pressures:** Dutch SDE++ "corrective
  factor" reductions and supply-chain delays make the H2 2025 COD
  timeline tight; capex reported at $1bn+.
- **Group capex allocation:** Guidance reduced to $20–22bn/year for
  2025–2028, with priority given to upstream and integrated gas;
  RES capex already −5% YoY in FY2024.
- **Quest blue H2 underperformance:** CCS capture rates of 39–48% vs
  90% target cast doubt on blue H2 economics.
- **Green Energy Oman export timing:** 1.8 Mt/year full phase
  2037; first revenue 2027 — highly long-dated.
- **H2 mobility viability:** California FCEV registrations 3,143 in
  2023 (~1% of EVs); Shell's retail exit reflects market weakness.
