## 1. Business overview

thyssenkrupp nucera AG & Co. KGaA is a global provider of
electrolysis technologies headquartered in Dortmund, Germany. The
company operates two segments: Chlor-Alkali (CA) and Green Hydrogen
(gH2). CA supplies industrial plants for chlorine and caustic soda
production. gH2 delivers alkaline water electrolysis (AWE) for
green-hydrogen production, with major projects including 700+ MW
for H2 Green Steel (Stegra) in Sweden and 400+ MW at NEOM in Saudi
Arabia. The company manufactures across alkali (scalum), advanced
bipolar-membrane chlor-alkali (BM2.7 v7, e-BiTAC v7), high-
temperature SOEC (pilot with Fraunhofer IKTS) and high-pressure
alkaline (IP acquired from Green Hydrogen Systems). Over 3 GW of
electrolyser capacity has been deployed across 600+ projects.

## 5. Hydrogen-specific operational KPIs

- **Installed / shipped capacity:** 3+ GW across 600+ projects. H2
  Green Steel 700+ MW; NEOM 400+ MW with 4+ million safe working
  hours. Q1 FY2025/26 gH2 sales −50% YoY to €77m.
- **Order backlog:** €606m at 30 Sept 2025; €489m at 31 Dec 2025.
- **Book-to-bill ratio (FY2024/25):** 0.41x (€348m intake / €845m
  revenue).
- **Manufacturing capacity:** Pilot high-temperature SOEC plant
  (Fraunhofer IKTS partnership) commenced Q1 FY2025/26; core
  manufacturing in Germany and Denmark.
- **Announced large contracts (past 12 months):** Cepsa/Moeve 300 MW
  reservation, FID confirmed March 2026 (Spain); largest CA new-build
  in company history, Middle East (Dec 2025); 260 MW FEED study in
  India (March 2026); Chlorum Solutions, Casa Grande, Arizona.

## 11. Key risks

1. **Green hydrogen order ramp delay:** Q1 gH2 intake −80% YoY to
   €5m; guidance revised down 28% on sales and EBIT in March 2026.
2. **Parent company overhang:** thyssenkrupp AG ~50.2% stake creates
   governance overhang and limits free float.
3. **Customer FID slowdown:** Stegra delayed to 2026; Cepsa/Moeve
   FID only confirmed March 2026; weak industrial capex cycle.
4. **H2 Green Steel concentration:** Stegra ~30% of gH2 backlog at
   FY2024/25 close; delivery delays would materially reduce
   near-term visibility.
5. **Rising project costs:** gH2 EBIT guidance widened to €(125m)–
   €(90m) loss band; limited cost pass-through visibility.
6. **Technology commoditisation:** alkaline becoming commoditised
   against ITM, Nel, Plug Power; SOEC still pre-commercial.
