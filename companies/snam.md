**Reporting currency:** EUR

**Segment:** Italy's leading natural-gas TSO; lead promoter of the
SoutH2 Corridor and the Italian hydrogen backbone.
**Data last updated:** 2026-04-13
**Latest full fiscal year reported:** FY2024
**Latest quarter reported:** H1 2025 (30 June 2025)

## 1. Business overview

Snam S.p.A. operates Italy's natural-gas transmission system (>32,500
km of pipelines), storage (17 bcm working capacity) and LNG
regasification. It is the lead promoter of the **SoutH2 Corridor**
(Algeria/Tunisia → Italy → Austria → Germany), a ~3,300 km
cross-border corridor targeting 4 Mt H2/year at the Austrian-German
border and designated a Project of Common Interest. Snam is
repurposing ~60% of its existing gas network into a 1,920 km Italian
hydrogen backbone. It holds a 33% stake in Industrie De Nora
(alkaline electrolyser electrodes, 34% JV partner in thyssenkrupp
nucera) and participates in the European Hydrogen Backbone initiative.

## 5. Hydrogen-specific operational KPIs

- **Italian hydrogen backbone length:** 1,920 km planned (~60%
  repurposed from existing gas network).
- **SoutH2 Corridor capacity:** 4 Mt H2/year from North Africa;
  ~3,300 km cross-border corridor with compressor stations under
  design.
- **De Nora stake:** 33% (alkaline electrolyser electrodes; 34% JV
  in thyssenkrupp nucera AWE).
- **Hydrogen blending target:** ~2% by 2030 in the existing gas grid.
- **CEF funding secured (2025):** €24m for Italian backbone and
  SoutH2 engineering / compression design.
- **Hydrogen backbone capex (2026–30 plan):** €200m (down from
  €380m in prior plan); part of wider €14.5bn cumulative capex to
  2032.
- **H2 pilots:** Sulmona compressor station (three Baker Hughes
  NovaLT12 units capable of 10% H2 blend).

## 11. Key hydrogen risks

- **Regulatory unbundling:** TSO involvement in production/storage
  may face ARERA scrutiny; EU unbundling rules could limit
  downstream H2 scope.
- **PCI / CEF funding timing:** €24m CEF conditional on regulatory
  approvals and co-financing availability.
- **Cross-border coordination:** SoutH2 requires synchronisation
  across Italy, Austria, Germany, Algeria, Tunisia.
- **Demand-side FID delays:** Italian industrial offtakers yet to
  commit; FID pushed to early 2030.
- **Natural-gas volume erosion:** structural gas demand decline
  only partly offset by H2 blending; stranded-asset risk on
  repurposed lines if H2 offtake below 4 Mt/yr.
- **De Nora / nucera technology risk:** alkaline cost reductions
  and PEM competition; JV governance complexity.
